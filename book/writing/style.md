# Writing: Style Guide

## Voice
Knowledgeable friend who has done this swap and is walking a buddy through it. Direct, honest, technically accurate. Not a manual — a guide written by someone who cares whether the reader succeeds.

## Tone rules
- Explain the why before the how — always. A reader who understands why a crank sensor matters will wire it correctly. One following steps blindly will get lost when something does not match.
- Respect mechanical knowledge. Do not explain what a socket wrench is. Do explain what a VR sensor is.
- Never condescend. The reader is capable — they are just new to this specific topic.
- Be direct about risk. If something is dangerous, say so clearly and early.
- Be honest about difficulty. If something is hard, say it is hard and explain what makes it hard.

## Sentence-level rules
- Active voice preferred — "the ECU grounds the relay coil" not "the relay coil is grounded by the ECU"
- Short sentences under load — complex technical steps get short sentences
- One instruction per sentence in procedures — never "do X and then Y"
- Numbers under 10 spelled out in prose; numbers 10 and above as numerals; always numerals for specs (3 ohms, 14.7:1, 0.5V)

## Acronyms
Define every acronym on first use in each chapter — do not rely on the glossary. Example: "The Automatic Shut Down relay (ASD relay) supplies 12V to the coil, injectors, and fuel pump simultaneously."

## Lists and tables
- Use numbered lists for sequential procedures
- Use bulleted lists for non-sequential items
- Use tables for specs, connector pinouts, and comparisons
- Never use a list where a sentence would do — lists fragment naturally flowing information

## Procedures
- State the goal of the procedure before the first step
- State required tools and materials before the first step
- Each step is one action
- Safety warnings appear before the step they apply to — never after
- End every procedure with a verification step — how does the reader confirm it worked?

## Length
- Chapters: 1,500–4,000 words depending on topic complexity
- No padding — if a chapter can be shorter, it should be
- Sidebars and callout boxes do not count toward chapter length — use them freely
