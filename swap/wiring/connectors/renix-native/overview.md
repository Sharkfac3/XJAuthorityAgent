# Renix-Native Connectors: Overview

## Identity
Era 1 (1988–1990) Renix sensors use proprietary connectors that are not shared with any later era. They are not Metri-Pack, not Packard, and not any other industry-standard family. They were designed by Renix/Bosch specifically for this application.

## Why this matters
- Replacement pigtails are increasingly difficult to source
- Standard terminal release tools do not fit
- Each sensor has a different body shape — there is no "one size fits all" within the Renix family
- Many swap builders replace Renix sensors with modern equivalents to escape the sourcing problem

## General guidance
- Before removing any Renix connector, photograph it from multiple angles
- If a tab breaks during removal, the connector body must be replaced — repair clips are not available
- When planning a swap on an Era 1 vehicle, budget for sensor replacement with modern equivalents — this eliminates the Renix connector sourcing problem entirely
- GM and Chrysler sensors with standard Metri-Pack connectors are the common modern substitutes
