# Lift Kits — XJ Cherokee Sourcing

## What this covers
Selecting a suspension lift kit for the XJ Cherokee. Does not cover installation procedure.

---

## Lift types by height

### 2" — spacer or mild spring lift
- **Spacer lift:** polyurethane or aluminum coil spring spacer sits on top of the existing spring; no new spring rate
- **Mild spring lift:** replacement coil springs with slightly higher free height
- Minimal geometry impact at this height; no UCAs required in most cases
- TeraFlex 2" shackle/spacer kits are a common low-cost entry point

### 3.5"–4.5" — most common trail-ready range
- Full coil spring replacement; most kits include shocks, track bar bracket, sway bar links, and bump stop extensions
- This height provides useful trail clearance while keeping driveline geometry manageable with included correction hardware
- **Adjustable upper control arms (UCAs)** are not always included but are commonly added when measured caster is out of spec after the lift — this is conditional, not a universal requirement
- Drop track bar bracket (included in most kits) is necessary to restore front axle centering

### 6"+ — long-arm territory
- Factory lower control arm mounts cannot maintain correct geometry at this height
- Requires long-arm kit that relocates lower control arm mounting points on the frame
- Significantly more fabrication and cost; not a bolt-on project
- An SYE (see `sye-kits.md`) is essentially mandatory at this height

---

## What a typical 3.5"–4.5" kit includes
- New coil springs (front and rear)
- Shocks (some kits; others list shocks separately)
- Drop track bar bracket (front)
- Extended sway bar end links
- Bump stop extensions
- Hardware

**What a kit does NOT fix:**
- Caster correction — adjustable UCAs required if caster measurement is outside spec after install
- Driveshaft angle — the rear slip yoke driveshaft becomes marginal at 3.5"+ combined with aggressive flex; an SYE (see `sye-kits.md`) is the solution
- Alignment — always realign after any lift

## Brands with confirmed XJ fitment

| Brand | Notes |
|-------|-------|
| **Rubicon Express** | Well-regarded community choice; RE6030 (3.5") and RE6307 (7.5" long-arm) are common XJ selections |
| **Old Man Emu (OME)** | Preferred for ride quality; available in 2" and approximately 3" heavy-duty configurations |
| **TeraFlex** | Budget and mid-range options; 2" spacer kit is a common starting point |
| **Rough Country** | Widely available and low cost; community feedback is consistently lukewarm on long-term durability, but kits do fit |

Community consensus places Rubicon Express and OME as the quality-first choices. Rough Country kits are functional but often described as a step toward something better.
