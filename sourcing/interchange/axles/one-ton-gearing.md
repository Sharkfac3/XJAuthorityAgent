# One-Ton Gearing Decisions

Gear ratio selection guide for XJ Cherokee builds running 2005+ Super Duty Dana 60 front and Sterling 10.50 rear axles.

---

## Rule: front and rear ratios must match

On a 4WD vehicle, the front and rear axle ratios **must be identical**. Mismatched ratios cause driveline binding in 4WD, destroy transfer case internals, and make the vehicle unsafe. There are no exceptions.

When re-gearing for a one-ton swap, always plan both axles as a pair.

---

## Ratio selection by tire size

These are community-consensus starting points for XJ builds with the 4.0L inline-six and NP231 transfer case. Adjust based on intended use (trail-heavy vs highway-heavy), transmission (AX-15/NV3550 manual vs AW4 auto), and personal preference.

| Tire size | Recommended ratio range | Notes |
|-----------|------------------------|-------|
| 35" | 4.56 | Minimum for acceptable highway RPM and trail crawl ratio |
| 37" | 4.88 | Most popular choice for 37s. Good balance of highway and trail. |
| 38–39" | 4.88–5.13 | 5.13 gives better crawl ratio but higher highway RPM |
| 40" | 5.13–5.38 | 5.13 minimum. 5.38 for trail-focused builds. |

### Highway RPM context (AW4 auto, NP231, 1:1 top gear)
- 4.56 on 37" tires ≈ 2,700 RPM at 65 mph
- 4.88 on 37" tires ≈ 2,900 RPM at 65 mph
- 5.13 on 40" tires ≈ 2,800 RPM at 65 mph
- 5.38 on 40" tires ≈ 2,950 RPM at 65 mph

The 4.0L is comfortable cruising at 2,700–3,000 RPM. Above 3,000 RPM at highway speed gets loud and fuel consumption increases noticeably.

## Ring-and-pinion availability

### Dana 60

| Ratio | Availability | Notes |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| 3.54 | Common | Stock donor ratio |
| 3.73 | Common | Stock donor ratio |
| 4.10 | Common | Stock donor ratio — often comes in the donor axle |
| 4.56 | Widely available | Yukon, Revolution, Motive — all major brands |
| 4.88 | Widely available | Most popular aftermarket ratio for one-ton builds |
| 5.13 | Available | Slightly less common but readily orderable |
| 5.38 | Available | Niche — check lead times |

**Carrier break: ~4.10.** Ratios 4.10 and numerically lower use the standard carrier. Ratios steeper than 4.10 (4.56, 4.88, 5.13, 5.38) require the thick/deep carrier. When buying gears for 4.56+, verify you have or are ordering the correct carrier.

### Sterling 10.50

| Ratio | Availability | Notes |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| 3.73 | Common | Stock donor ratio |
| 4.10 | Common | Stock donor ratio |
| 4.56 | Available | Yukon, Motive |
| 4.88 | Available | Most common aftermarket choice |
| 5.13 | Available | Fewer brands — verify supplier stock |
| 5.38 | Limited | Specialty order — longer lead time and higher cost |

**Carrier break: ~3.73.** Ratios 3.73 and numerically lower use the standard carrier. Ratios steeper than 3.73 (4.10, 4.56, 4.88, 5.13, 5.38) require the thick/deep carrier. For any XJ one-ton build running 37"+ tires, you will need the high-ratio carrier.

## Locker and limited-slip options

### Dana 60

| Type | Product examples | Notes |
|------|-----------------|-------|
| Selectable (air) | ARB RD166 | Gold standard. Full lock on demand, open on street. Requires onboard air. |
| Selectable (electric) | Eaton E-Locker | Electric actuation — no air system needed. |
| Automatic | Detroit Truetrac | Gear-driven limited slip. Quiet, strong, always active. |
| Full-case | Detroit Locker | Always locked. Tire chirp on turns. Best for dedicated trail rigs. |
| Lunchbox | Spartan, Aussie Locker | Drop-in to open carrier. Budget option. |

### Sterling 10.50

| Type | Product examples | Notes |
|------|-----------------|-------|
| Selectable (air) | ARB RD159 | Best option for street/trail dual use. |
| Automatic | Detroit Truetrac | Good street manners, adequate trail traction. |
| Full-case | Detroit Locker | Always locked. Trail-focused. |

Fewer aftermarket locker options exist for the Sterling 10.50 compared to the Dana 60. ARB and Detroit are the primary brands with proven Sterling support.
