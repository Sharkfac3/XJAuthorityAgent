# Junkyard Upgrades — Axles

Donor-vehicle axle assemblies and components that interchange with the XJ Cherokee.
Sourced from community-compiled junkyard upgrade threads (NAXJA, JeepForum, CherokeeForam, Pirate4x4).

---

## Complete Axle Assemblies

### Ford 8.8 (Explorer)
- **Donor:** 1995–2001 Ford Explorer
- **Fits:** All XJ years (with fabrication)
- **Gain:** 4.10 gears, LSD, rear disc brakes — all in one package. Strength comparable to or exceeding Dana 44.
- **Fitment notes:** Requires welding spring perches and shock mounts to the Explorer housing. Explorer 8.8 is approximately 1.5" narrower total than XJ width — generally considered ideal. The '97+ Explorers are the most commonly sourced.

### XJ Dana 44 (Tow Package)
- **Donor:** 1987–1989 Jeep Cherokee XJ with factory Towing Package
- **Fits:** All XJ years
- **Gain:** Factory Dana 44 rear — significant strength upgrade over Dana 35
- **Fitment notes:** Direct bolt-in to any XJ. Not all 87–89 XJs have it — only those with the tow package. Relatively rare find.

### XJ Chrysler 8.25 (97+)
- **Donor:** 1997–2001 Jeep Cherokee XJ
- **Fits:** All XJ years (bolt-in for 97+; earlier years may need minor bracket work)
- **Gain:** 29-spline axle shafts — strength comparable to Dana 44. Significant upgrade over the 27-spline Dana 35.
- **Fitment notes:** Direct bolt-in for 97–01 XJs. The 8.25 is the best stock rear axle offered in the XJ.

## Axle Shafts

### 297-Spec Front Axle Shafts
- **Donor:** 1995+ XJ or any ABS-equipped XJ Cherokee
- **Fits:** All XJ Dana 30 front axles
- **Gain:** Stronger 297-size U-joints vs. earlier 260-size
- **Fitment notes:** Direct swap into any XJ Dana 30. The 95+ / ABS shafts use the larger 297 U-joint — a worthwhile upgrade for any XJ running larger tires or wheeling hard.

## Rear Axle Shafts

### Dodge Dakota Rear Shafts
- **Donor:** Dodge Dakota
- **Fits:** XJ with SYE need or longer shaft requirement
- **Gain:** Alternative to traditional SYE — longer driveshaft options
- **Fitment notes:** Front shaft from a Grand Cherokee is also used in some SYE configurations. Measure carefully — multiple lengths available across Dakota model years.

## Gearing

### XJ I-4 / V6 Axles (for Regearing)
- **Donor:** XJ Cherokee with 2.5L I-4 or 2.8L V6
- **Fits:** All XJ axle housings of the same type
- **Gain:** 4.10 gears (some have 4.56) — desirable ratio for larger tires
- **Fitment notes:** The I-4 and V6 XJs often came with 4.10 or steeper gears. Some also have vacuum disconnects on the front axle. Pull the whole carrier or just the ring-and-pinion set.
