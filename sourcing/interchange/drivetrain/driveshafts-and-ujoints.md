# Driveshafts and U-Joints — Interchange (XJ Cherokee)

## What this covers
Driveshaft and u-joint sourcing for stock-length replacement and custom lifted applications.

---

## Rear driveshaft — stock length

Stock-length rear driveshafts from any same-year XJ Cherokee interchange directly. If sourcing from a junkyard, match the year range and verify slip yoke vs. fixed flange output (relevant after a SYE conversion — see note below).

---

## Custom rear driveshaft — lifted applications

**Community standard: Tom Woods Custom Drive Shafts (4xshaft.com)**

For any XJ running an SYE or a lift that has changed the required driveshaft length, Tom Woods custom driveshafts are the consistent community choice. Characteristics:
- Built to exact customer-specified length
- Double-cardan (CV) rear shaft design handles steep driveshaft angles without vibration
- Greaseable joints
- Ships within one business day from order
- Available for both slip yoke and fixed-flange (post-SYE) applications

When ordering, measure carefully — the correct length depends on lift height and SYE type. Tom Woods provides measurement instructions.

---

## U-Joints

**OEM-spec: Dana Spicer**

Dana Spicer (Spicer brand, now owned by Dana Incorporated) is the OEM-spec u-joint supplier for XJ driveline components. Spicer u-joints are available in standard and **greaseable** versions.

**Greaseable u-joints are preferred for off-road use.** Sealed u-joints cannot be maintained once installed; greaseable versions extend service life significantly on vehicles that see mud, water crossings, and trail use.

Relevant Spicer part families:
- Front axle u-joints (inner and outer birfield/u-joint): 5-760X series
- Rear driveshaft: 5-153X series (greaseable)
- Front driveshaft CV joint: 5-1310-1X series

**Spicer Extreme (nickel chromoly SAE-4340 alloy)** u-joints are available for high-stress applications and are worth considering on lifted builds or tow rigs.

---

## Front driveshaft

The front driveshaft on most XJ configurations is shorter and operates at a more favorable angle than the rear. Stock-length front driveshafts from same-year XJs interchange. A custom front shaft is less often needed than a custom rear shaft.
