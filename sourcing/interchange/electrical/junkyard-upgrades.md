# Junkyard Upgrades — Electrical

Donor-vehicle electrical components that interchange with the XJ Cherokee.
Sourced from community-compiled junkyard upgrade threads (NAXJA, JeepForum, CherokeeForam, Pirate4x4).

---

## Alternators

### 136A High-Output Alternator
- **Donor:** 1998 Jeep Grand Cherokee (318 V8), or 1997–1998 Dodge Dakota, Durango, Ram, or Full-Size Van
- **Fits:** All XJ years
- **Gain:** 136 amps vs. 90A (early XJ) or 117A (late XJ) stock — more capacity for accessories, winches, lights
- **Fitment notes:** Mopar unit. Slight grinding of the XJ alternator bracket may be required for clearance. May need a larger gauge charge wire if upgrading from a 90A unit. One of the most common and recommended electrical upgrades.

### 117A Dakota Alternator
- **Donor:** Dodge Dakota 2.5L
- **Fits:** All XJ years
- **Gain:** 117 amps — ~27 amps over the early XJ stock 90A alternator
- **Fitment notes:** Required absolutely no modification at all — true bolt-in. Good option when a 136A unit isn't available at the yard.

## Cruise Control

### XJ Cruise Control System
- **Donor:** XJ Cherokee (various years with cruise option)
- **Fits:** XJ without factory cruise control
- **Gain:** Cruise control add-on — servo, switches, clockspring
- **Fitment notes:** ~$30 at a junkyard. Year-specific compatibility — the clockspring and steering column switches must match the recipient XJ's year range. Typically involves the servo (on firewall), cable, clockspring, and stalk switch.

## Antenna

### GM Power Antenna
- **Donor:** Various GM vehicles with power antenna
- **Fits:** All XJ years
- **Gain:** Replaces broken or stuck XJ antenna with a power-retractable unit
- **Fitment notes:** Minor modification to the XJ fender mounting hole. Wiring is straightforward — power, ground, and trigger from the radio.

## Instrument Cluster

### Full-Gauge Instrument Cluster
- **Donor:** XJ Cherokee with full gauge cluster (vs. idiot-light version)
- **Fits:** XJ with idiot-light cluster (same generation)
- **Gain:** Oil pressure gauge, voltmeter, and temperature gauge replace warning lights
- **Fitment notes:** The difference between idiot-light and full-gauge clusters is not electrical — the gauges are wired the same, just terminated differently at the cluster. Should interchange between model years within the same generation. Verify connector match before pulling.

## Power Steering Cooling

### AC Condenser as PS Cooler
- **Donor:** XJ Cherokee with factory AC (use the AC cooler/condenser lines)
- **Fits:** XJ without AC (or with AC removed for on-board air)
- **Gain:** Eliminates low-speed power steering whine by cooling PS fluid
- **Fitment notes:** If you've scrapped your Renix AC system for on-board air, repurpose the existing AC cooler by plumbing it into the power steering return line. Free cooling with existing hardware.
