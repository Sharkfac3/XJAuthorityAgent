# Battery, Alternator, and Starter — Interchange (XJ Cherokee)

## What this covers
Sourcing and interchange guidance for the XJ Cherokee battery, alternator, and starter. For high-output alternator donor upgrades, see [`junkyard-upgrades.md`](junkyard-upgrades.md).

---

## Battery

**OEM group size: Group 58**

Group 58 is the stock battery for most XJ configurations. Two common upgrade paths exist:

| Group | Fitment notes |
|-------|--------------|
| Group 58 | OEM; direct fit |
| Group 34 | Popular upgrade; fits the battery tray with minimal or no modification; offers significantly more cold cranking amps and reserve capacity; recommended by the community for builds with winches or accessories |
| Group 65 | Requires battery box modification; clearance issues with AC bracket and transmission cooler lines on some years; not a straightforward swap |

**Brand:** Any quality battery in the correct group size is acceptable. No specific brand dominates XJ community recommendations — focus on CCA (cold cranking amps) rating and battery group.

---

## Alternator

**OEM supplier: Denso (Nippondenso)**

Denso is the confirmed OEM alternator supplier for 1991–2001 XJ Cherokees. The 1991+ HO-era alternators are externally regulated via a PWM signal from the PCM.

**Remanufactured alternators:** Community experience is mixed. Some reman units from auto parts stores fail early. If using a reman, buy from a store with a solid warranty policy.

**High-output alternator upgrade:** The 136A Mopar alternator from a 1998 Grand Cherokee (318 V8) or comparable Dodge truck is the community-standard high-output upgrade for XJ. See [`junkyard-upgrades.md`](junkyard-upgrades.md) for fitment details.

Aftermarket new alternators (Powermaster and similar) are available for XJ applications and sidestep the reman quality lottery at higher cost.

---

## Starter

**OEM supplier: Mitsubishi**

The stock starter on the XJ 4.0L is a Mitsubishi unit — not Nippondenso. (Nippondenso supplies alternators; Mitsubishi supplies starters.) Mitsubishi starters from any model year of 4.0L XJ interchange freely. The main variation is the solenoid trigger lead: some use a 4mm threaded stud, others use a 1/4" spade terminal.

**Remanufactured starters:** Same quality caveats as alternators — reman units vary. OEM-spec reman from a quality supplier (or a pull-out Mitsubishi unit from a same-era XJ or ZJ) is more reliable than the cheapest shelf option.

**High-torque starter:** Aftermarket high-torque starters are available for XJ applications and are worthwhile on a high-compression built engine. Powermaster is one common brand.
