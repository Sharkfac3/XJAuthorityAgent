# Engine Gaskets — OEM vs. Aftermarket (XJ Cherokee 4.0L)

## What this covers
Gasket selection for the XJ 4.0L inline-six. Head gasket, exhaust manifold gasket, and intake manifold gasket.

---

## Head gasket

**Community standard: Fel-Pro**

Fel-Pro is the consistent community recommendation on NAXJA and JeepForum for the 4.0L head gasket. Two variants are relevant:

| Type | Part (ref) | Use case |
|------|-----------|----------|
| MLS (multi-layer steel) | HS 26211 PT (PermaTorque) | Stock or built engine; requires a very flat, smooth deck surface |
| Composite | HS 9076 PT-4 | Stock replacement; tolerates slightly rougher deck surfaces |

**MLS is preferred for built engines.** It provides a superior seal under higher cylinder pressures. However, MLS requires that the head and block deck surfaces are machined flat and smooth — it does not conform to surface irregularities the way composite gaskets do.

For a straightforward head gasket replacement on a stock 4.0L, either MLS or composite Fel-Pro is acceptable. If the head has been resurfaced, use MLS.

---

## Exhaust manifold gasket

The 4.0L exhaust manifold is notorious for cracking. When a cracked or warped manifold is being reused (rather than replaced), a standard-style gasket may not seal adequately against the distorted surface.

**Remflex (part 10-001 for the 4.0L)** is the community-recommended solution in this situation:
- Thick graphite construction that compresses and fills voids on warped surfaces
- Does not require retorquing after installation (unlike stock gaskets)
- Handles cracked manifold surfaces better than stock-style paper or composite gaskets

If the manifold is new or undamaged, a standard Fel-Pro exhaust manifold gasket is fine.

See also: [`../../../vehicle/systems/engine/exhaust-manifold.md`](../../../vehicle/systems/engine/exhaust-manifold.md)

---

## Intake manifold gasket

Fel-Pro stock replacement is the standard choice. No strong community preference between Fel-Pro and other brands here — the intake manifold does not experience the thermal cycling extremes of the exhaust side.
