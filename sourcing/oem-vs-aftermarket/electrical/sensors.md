# Engine Sensors — OEM vs. Aftermarket (XJ Cherokee 4.0L)

## What this covers
Sensor sourcing guidance for the XJ 4.0L. CPS, TPS, CTS/IAT, and O2 sensors.

---

## CPS — Crankshaft Position Sensor

**First choice: Standard Motor Products (SMP)**

The OEM Mopar CPS (part 56026882 for 1993–1996) has been discontinued and is no longer readily available. Standard Motor Products is the practical community-recommended alternative and has demonstrated multi-year reliability in NAXJA threads.

**Why this matters:** Cheap offshore CPS sensors are a documented cause of intermittent no-start conditions on the XJ 4.0L. The CPS signal is critical to crank timing — a marginal sensor may start fine when cold but fail when hot, causing unpredictable no-start behavior. This is one of the most failure-prone sensors on the 4.0L and worth buying quality.

Delphi is another acceptable option when SMP is unavailable.

---

## TPS — Throttle Position Sensor

**Recommended: Standard Motor Products (SMP)**

SMP TPS units are widely available and consistently recommended. Cheap TPS replacements are documented to have dead spots in the sweep, causing stumbling, erratic idle, or surge. At $26–$31 for SMP, there is no reason to buy the cheapest available unit.

---

## CTS — Coolant Temperature Sensor / IAT — Intake Air Temp Sensor

Both are standard NTC (negative temperature coefficient) thermistors. Most aftermarket replacements are acceptable for these sensors. No strong community preference — OEM-spec replacements from any name-brand supplier (Delphi, Standard, Mopar where available) are fine.

---

## O2 Sensors

**Recommended: Denso or NTK/NGK**

- **Denso:** Well-regarded; aluminum oxide trap layer provides contamination protection; consistent community reports of good results
- **NTK/NGK:** OEM supplier for many applications; highly regarded in community discussions

**Bosch — qualified caution:** Bosch O2 sensors are sold as premium, but there are documented community reports of PCM compatibility issues on XJ applications. This is not universal, but enough forum discussion exists (JeepForum, Jeep Garage) to warrant caution. Denso or NTK is the safer choice.

This caution applies particularly to a tuned or modified setup where the O2 signal plays a larger role. On a fully stock 4.0L the risk is lower but Denso/NTK is still the better choice.
