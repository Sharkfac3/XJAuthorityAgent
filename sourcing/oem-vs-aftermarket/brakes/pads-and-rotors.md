# Brake Pads and Rotors — OEM vs. Aftermarket (XJ Cherokee)

## What this covers
Front and rear brake pad and rotor selection for the XJ Cherokee. Does not cover installation procedure.

---

## Front rotors

Stock front rotor size replacement is straightforward. Multiple well-regarded brands fit without modification.

**Recommended brands:** EBC, Centric, PowerStop

- **EBC:** Strong community reputation for quality; consistent positive feedback on NAXJA
- **Centric:** Highly recommended; "great quality" is the consistent characterization in NAXJA brake threads
- **PowerStop:** Used successfully by many XJ owners; some reports of early wear — acceptable choice but inspect regularly
- **Wagner:** Economy-tier option; acceptable for budget builds

Slotted or dimpled rotors are preferred by the off-road community for better heat dissipation and gas release. Drilled rotors are less common for XJ trail use.

---

## Brake pad compound

### Semi-metallic — preferred for street/trail mixed use
- Better initial bite — works effectively before pads fully warm up
- Better thermal transfer to the rotor — helps manage heat under hard use
- The appropriate choice for any XJ that sees off-road or trail use

### Ceramic — not recommended for off-road use
- Ceramic pads insulate thermally rather than transferring heat, which can cause rotor warping under repeated heavy braking
- Reduced initial bite — ceramic pads need to reach operating temperature before they work well
- Produce less brake dust (a street benefit), but that benefit is irrelevant off-road
- Community consensus on forums like NAXJA and CherokeeForam is consistent: avoid ceramic for off-road or mixed-use XJs

---

## Rear drums

The XJ uses rear drums on most configurations. Standard replacement drums and hardware are a commodity — no strong community preference between name-brand options. Avoid the cheapest no-name hardware kits; OEM-spec wheel cylinders and springs are inexpensive and reliable.
