# Serpentine Belt — Jeep Cherokee XJ

## Overview

The XJ 4.0L uses a single serpentine belt driving all accessory components. Belt part numbers vary by accessory configuration (A/C vs. non-A/C, power steering) — always order by year, engine, and accessory equipment.

## Tensioner

| Era | Tensioner type |
|---|---|
| 1987–1990 (Renix) | Manual adjustment — no automatic tensioner |
| 1991–2001 (HO) | Automatic spring-loaded tensioner |

On automatic-tensioner vehicles, if the belt cannot be tensioned correctly by the factory tensioner, inspect the tensioner itself for wear or spring fatigue before replacing the belt.

## Belt routing

A routing diagram is printed on a decal in the engine bay on most XJs. If the decal is missing, consult the Factory Service Manual for the specific year and accessory configuration. Routing varies between A/C and non-A/C vehicles.

## Inspection criteria

Replace on any of the following:
- Visible cracking across the ribs (more than superficial surface cracks)
- Fraying at the edges
- Glazing (shiny rib surface indicating slippage)
- Missing rib chunks
- Age — most manufacturers recommend replacement at 60,000–100,000 miles regardless of appearance on older rubber
