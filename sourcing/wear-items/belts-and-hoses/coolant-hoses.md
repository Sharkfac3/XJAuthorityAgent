# Coolant Hoses — Jeep Cherokee XJ

## Hose list

- Upper radiator hose (thermostat housing to radiator inlet)
- Lower radiator hose (radiator outlet to water pump)
- Heater hoses (two — supply and return to heater core)
- Bypass hose (short hose near thermostat housing on some years)
- Overflow hose (radiator filler neck to overflow bottle)

Order by year — hose profiles and thermostat housing geometry differ between the Renix era (1987–1990) and the HO era (1991–2001).

## Inspection criteria

Replace on any of the following:
- **Soft spots or bulging** — internal delamination, collapse risk under pressure
- **Swelling at the collar** (where hose meets fitting) — common failure point; cracks and leaks under heat/pressure cycling
- **Surface cracking** — surface crazing on aged rubber indicates the hose is past its service life
- **Collapse** — lower radiator hose collapse at operating temperature (lower hose may need a wire spring insert if the internal spring is missing or corroded)
- Age — hoses on 20+ year old vehicles should be inspected at every service and replaced proactively if original

## Notes

- The XJ cooling system runs at ~16 PSI. A hose that looks intact at cold idle can blow under pressure. Squeeze test: a healthy hose is firm with some give; a deteriorating hose feels either very soft (internal breakdown) or very hard and brittle (age-hardened).
- Heater hose fittings at the firewall are a common leak point on high-mileage XJs. Inspect the fitting area closely.
