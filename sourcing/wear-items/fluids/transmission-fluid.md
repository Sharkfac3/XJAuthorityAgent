# Transmission Fluid — Jeep Cherokee XJ

## AW4 Automatic (1987–2001)

**Fluid:** Dexron III / Mercon compatible ATF.

**Do not use ATF+4.** The AW4 is an Aisin-Warner unit, not a Chrysler design. It was built to the Dexron spec. Community experience on NAXJA and JeepForum consistently documents slipping and shift problems when ATF+4 is used in AW4 units that have been on Dexron — especially over 100,000 miles. A 2007 Chrysler TSB approved ATF+4 for the AW4, but the forums consider this a parts-logistics decision, not an engineering endorsement. Stick with Dexron-compatible ATF.

True licensed Dexron III is no longer produced. Use a current Dex/Merc-compatible fluid (Valvoline MaxLife, Mobil 1 Synthetic ATF, Amsoil Multi-Vehicle ATF). These are widely available and community-confirmed in the AW4.

**Pan service:** Drop pan, replace flat gasket (no RTV), replace filter. See [`../../filters/transmission.md`](../../filters/transmission.md).

## AX-15 Manual (1989–2001, behind 4.0L)

**Fluid:** 75W-90 **GL-4** gear oil. Redline MT-90 is the community's top pick. Alternatively, 10W-30 synthetic motor oil is the updated Chrysler recommendation and works well.

**Do not use GL-5.** GL-5 gear oil contains sulfur-based EP additives that attack the bronze synchronizer rings in the AX-15. If using a GL-5 fluid, confirm the label explicitly states "safe for yellow metals / bronze synchros" — most conventional GL-5 does not.

**Do not use Pennzoil Synchromesh MTF** as a substitute — that fluid is spec'd for the NV3550, not the AX-15. Mixed reports of hard, notchy shifts when used in the AX-15.

**Capacity:** ~3.3 quarts.

---

## AX-5 Manual (1988–1994, behind 2.5L)

**Fluid:** Same spec as AX-15 — 75W-90 GL-4 or 10W-30 motor oil. Same GL-5 warning applies.

## BA-10/5 Manual (1988–1989, Peugeot 5-speed)

**Fluid:** 75W-90 GL-5 gear oil. Redline 75W-90 NS and Pennzoil Synchromesh are community-recommended options.

Note: Some older references cite Dexron ATF for the BA-10/5. This is incorrect for long-term use. ATF is sometimes used as a temporary flush/rinse, but ATF is antagonistic to the seals in this gearbox and should not be left in as a permanent fill.

**Fill plug warning:** The BA-10/5 has three plugs. The actual fill/check hole is on the passenger side above the transmission crossmember — not the 5th-gear switch plug further forward. Filling from the wrong hole leaves the transmission nearly 1 quart low, which is a primary cause of BA-10/5 failure.
