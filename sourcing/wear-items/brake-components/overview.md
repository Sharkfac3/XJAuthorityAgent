# Brake Components Overview — Jeep Cherokee XJ

## Stock configuration

**All 1987–2001 XJs left the factory with front disc / rear drum brakes.**

There was no factory rear disc option on the XJ. Rear disc is an aftermarket conversion (common donor: KJ Liberty or ZJ Grand Cherokee calipers and rotors). If a vehicle has rear discs, it is an aftermarket installation.

| Position | Type | Years |
|---|---|---|
| Front | Disc | All XJ years |
| Rear | Drum | All 1987–2001 XJ years (factory) |

ABS was available on certain trim levels and years. ABS does not change the fundamental disc/drum configuration — it only adds wheel speed sensors and an ABS modulator to the hydraulic circuit.

## Brake fluid

**DOT 3** is the factory spec for all XJ years.

DOT 4 is a compatible upgrade — higher wet boiling point, useful under sustained braking. DOT 3 and DOT 4 are fully compatible and can be mixed.

**Do not use DOT 5.** DOT 5 is silicone-based and is not compatible with DOT 3/4 systems. It will cause seal damage and brake failure.

## Pad compound notes

- **Street pads (semi-metallic or organic):** Appropriate for daily driving and mild trail use.
- **Ceramic pads:** Common OEM replacement choice. Produce less rotor dust. However, ceramic compounds can fade more quickly under sustained hard braking or trail use — not ideal for loaded off-road driving with frequent brake application.
- **Semi-metallic or carbon-metallic pads:** Better fade resistance for trail use. More rotor wear and more brake dust than ceramic.

## Rotor dimensions — front (stock)

Stock front rotor dimensions vary by year and whether the vehicle is equipped with ABS. Confirm by year before ordering — do not assume a single spec covers the full XJ run.

*Specific rotor thickness specifications: needs verification against FSM by year. Use a caliper to measure existing rotors and compare against the minimum thickness specification stamped on the rotor hat.*

## Shoe and drum — rear

Rear drum brakes are self-adjusting on the XJ. Common service items: brake shoes, drums, wheel cylinders, hardware kit (springs, adjusters). Drums should be measured for maximum wear diameter — the spec is stamped on the drum itself.
