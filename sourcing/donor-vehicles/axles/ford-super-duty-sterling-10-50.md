# Donor: 2005+ Ford Super Duty — Sterling 10.25 / 10.50 Rear Axle

Donor identification and junkyard guidance for pulling a Sterling rear axle from a 2005+ Ford F-250 / F-350 Super Duty for use under a Jeep Cherokee XJ.

---

## Why this donor

The Sterling 10.50 is the natural rear axle companion to the Super Duty Dana 60 front:
- **Massive strength** — 10.50" ring gear with full-float option. Dramatically overbuilt for an XJ, which means reliability under 37–40" tires and hard use.
- **Full-float available** — full-float axles allow shaft removal without losing the wheel. Easier service, no risk of wheel separation on shaft failure.
- **Matched donor** — pulling front and rear from the same truck simplifies sourcing and often means matched gear ratios.
- **Community proven** — common pairing with the Dana 60 front in one-ton XJ builds.

## Sterling 10.25 vs 10.50

| Spec | Sterling 10.25 | Sterling 10.50 |
|------|---------------|----------------|
| Ring gear diameter | 10.25" | 10.50" |
| Axle type | Semi-float | Full-float (F-250 SRW), Semi-float (some F-150 HD) |
| Axle shaft spline count | 35-spline | 35-spline |
| Carrier strength | Good | **Better** — stronger carrier, larger bearings |
| Pinion shaft diameter | 1.625" | 1.875" |
| Typical donor | 1999–2004 F-250/F-350, some earlier | **2005+ F-250/F-350** |
| Recommendation | Acceptable if 10.50 unavailable | **Preferred** |

**Target the Sterling 10.50 from 2005+ trucks.** Same housing dimensions, stronger internals. The 10.25 is an older design found in earlier Super Dutys and some E-series vans.

## Target vehicles

| Year range | Model | Rear axle | Notes |
|------------|-------|-----------|-------|
| 2005–2010 | F-250 SRW 4WD | Sterling 10.50 full-float | **Primary target.** Most common, best documented. |
| 2005–2010 | F-350 SRW 4WD | Sterling 10.50 full-float | Same axle as F-250 SRW. |
| 2005–2010 | F-350 DRW 4WD | Sterling 10.50 full-float | Wider housing — more narrowing needed. |
| 2011+ | F-250 / F-350 | Sterling 10.50 or AAM 10.5 | Ford transitioned to AAM in some models. Verify before pulling. |

### SRW vs DRW

| Variant | Housing width (WMS-to-WMS) | Recommendation |
|---------|---------------------------|----------------|
| SRW | ~66" | **Preferred.** Less narrowing for XJ fitment. |
| DRW | ~72" | Significantly wider. More narrowing, more fabrication. Avoid if possible. |

### Full-float vs semi-float identification

- **Full-float:** Hub stands proud of the axle tube end. Large spindle nut visible on the hub face. Axle shaft pulls straight out without removing the wheel.
- **Semi-float:** Hub is flush with or recessed into the axle tube end. Shaft is retained by C-clips or bolts inside the differential.

**All 2005+ F-250/F-350 SRW rear axles are full-float.** If you find a semi-float Sterling at a junkyard in a Super Duty, it's likely a 10.25 from an older truck or has been swapped — verify.

## What to pull

### Must-have
- Complete housing with tubes and center section
- Carrier and ring-and-pinion
- Both axle shafts
- Both hub assemblies (full-float hubs with spindle nuts and lock washers)
- Brake rotors, calipers, and brackets
- Differential cover
- Vent hose

### Nice to have
- E-brake hardware (drum-in-hat assembly inside the rear rotors on full-float models)
- E-brake cables (for reference — XJ routing will differ)
- ABS tone rings and sensors
- Donor brake lines and fittings
- Pinion yoke or flange (depending on driveshaft plan)

### Do not need
- Coil springs or leaf springs from the donor (XJ leaf springs will be used)
- Shock mounts (will be fabricated for XJ geometry)
- Sway bar and mounts

## Junkyard identification

### Visual tells
- **Full-float hubs** — large hubs with a spindle nut on the face, standing proud of the tube end
- **Sterling diff cover** — distinctive shape, roughly 12" diameter, 12 bolts, often has a "Sterling" or "Ford" logo
- **Housing size** — noticeably larger tubes and center section than half-ton axles
- **8-lug pattern** — 8 on 170mm, matching the Dana 60 front

### Tag decoding
- The axle tag is located on the housing, usually on the driver-side tube near the center section
- Tag includes: axle ratio, date code, BOM number, and limited-slip indicator
- **"L" on the tag** = limited-slip differential equipped
- **Ratio** printed directly on the tag (e.g., "3.73", "4.10")

### Common factory ratios

| Ratio | Typical application |
|-------|-------------------|
| 3.73 | Gas engine, standard |
| 4.10 | Gas tow package / diesel standard |
| 4.30 | Diesel, some configurations |
| 3.55 | Gas, light-duty spec |

**Same note as the front:** for 37–40" tires on an XJ, you'll re-gear to 4.56–5.13 regardless. Don't reject a good housing over factory gears.
