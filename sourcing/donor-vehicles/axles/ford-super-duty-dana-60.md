# Donor: 2005+ Ford Super Duty — Dana 60 Front Axle

Donor identification and junkyard guidance for pulling a Dana 60 front axle from a 2005+ Ford F-250 / F-350 Super Duty for use under a Jeep Cherokee XJ.

---

## Why this donor

The 2005+ Super Duty Dana 60 is the most accessible modern one-ton front axle for an XJ swap:
- **Ball-joint knuckles** — all 2005+ Super Duty Dana 60s are ball-joint, not kingpin. Easier to service, lighter, widely supported by aftermarket high-steer arms.
- **Coil-spring housing** — the coil buckets will be removed and replaced with leaf spring perches, but the housing itself is extremely strong.
- **Unit bearings** — press-in style hubs with unitized bearings. Simple, reliable, no manual locking hubs.
- **8-lug wheel pattern** — 8 on 170mm. Wide tire and wheel selection.
- **Community proven** — this is the most common one-ton front swap for XJs and other leaf-sprung rigs.

## Target vehicles

| Year range | Model | Notes |
|------------|-------|-------|
| 2005–2010 | F-250 / F-350 4WD | Coil-spring Dana 60, ball-joint knuckles. Most common donor years. |
| 2011–2016 | F-250 / F-350 4WD | Same basic Dana 60 housing. Minor knuckle and hub changes — still compatible. |
| 2017+ | F-250 / F-350 4WD | Dana/AAM Super 60. Housing design changes — verify fitment of aftermarket components before pulling. |

### SRW vs DRW

| Variant | Housing width (WMS-to-WMS) | Recommendation |
|---------|---------------------------|----------------|
| SRW (Single Rear Wheel) | ~71" | **Preferred.** Narrower housing means less material to remove when narrowing for XJ width. |
| DRW (Dual Rear Wheel) | ~75" | Wider. More narrowing required. Only use if SRW is unavailable. |

**Always target SRW donors.** The front axle width is the same regardless of SRW/DRW truck trim, but SRW trucks are more common at junkyards and the rear Sterling (if pulling a matched set) will be narrower on SRW.

## What to pull

### Must-have
- Complete housing with inner C, tubes, and differential carrier
- Both knuckles with ball joints
- Both unit bearing hubs
- Both axle shafts (inner and outer)
- Tie rod assembly (inner and outer tie rods)
- Brake rotors and calipers (with brackets)
- Ring-and-pinion set (if it has the gear ratio you want)
- Differential cover
- Vent hose

### Nice to have
- Donor brake lines and fittings (for reference/mock-up)
- Steering knuckle hardware (nuts, cotter pins)
- ABS tone rings and sensors (may be useful depending on XJ ABS integration plan)

### Do not need
- Coil springs (XJ uses leaf springs — the coil buckets will be cut off)
- Radius arms and brackets (XJ is leaf-sprung — these get cut off the housing)
- Track bar and bracket (XJ uses a different suspension geometry)
- Sway bar mounts

## Junkyard identification

### Visual tells
- **Coil spring buckets** welded to the top of the housing tubes — large, obvious round cups
- **Radius arm mounts** welded to the bottom of the tubes — heavy cast brackets pointing rearward
- **Ball-joint knuckles** — look for the upper and lower ball joint taper, not the large kingpin caps of older Dana 60s
- **8-lug hubs** — count the studs (8), pattern is 8 on 170mm
- **Housing casting** — look for "DANA" or "SPICER" cast into the center section or stamped on the diff cover

### Tag and casting numbers
- The Bill of Materials (BOM) tag is on the housing, typically on the passenger-side tube near the center section
- Tag includes: ratio, date of manufacture, and BOM number
- Dana 60 center section casting: look for "60" in the casting number
- Common ring gear bolt count: 12 bolts

### Ratio identification
Common Super Duty ratios from the factory:

| Ratio | Typical application |
|-------|-------------------|
| 3.73 | Gas engine, standard |
| 4.10 | Gas engine, tow package / diesel common |
| 4.30 | Diesel, some configurations |
| 3.55 | Gas, light-duty spec |

**For XJ one-ton builds running 37–40" tires:** you will almost certainly re-gear to 4.56, 4.88, or 5.13 regardless of the donor ratio. Don't pass up a good housing over the wrong gears — the ring-and-pinion is a replaceable item.
