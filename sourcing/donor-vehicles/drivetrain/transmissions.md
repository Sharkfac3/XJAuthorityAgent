# Transmission and Transfer Case Donors — XJ Cherokee

## What this covers
Which donor vehicles supply compatible AW4, AX-15, and NP231 components. Does not cover installation.

---

## AW4 Automatic Transmission

The AW4 (Aisin Warner 4-speed automatic) was used in XJ Cherokees from 1987–2001 paired with the 4.0L. It is the most common XJ transmission.

**Compatible donors:**

| Donor | Years | Notes |
|-------|-------|-------|
| XJ Cherokee | 1987–2001 | All 4.0L manual-equipped — highly interchangeable; preferred donor |
| MJ Comanche | 1987–1992 | 4.0L; AW4 compatible |
| ZJ Grand Cherokee | **1993 only** | 1993 ZJ with 4.0L is compatible; other ZJ years and engines are not a reliable match |

**Important:** Only the 1993 ZJ with the 4.0L is a straightforward AW4 donor. Other ZJ years used different transmission variants or TCU setups. 1998–2001 XJ TCUs have differences in sensor configuration and tailhousing from earlier units — verify compatibility before swapping late-model units into early-model XJs.

For detailed AW4 interchange notes, see: [`../../interchange/drivetrain/junkyard-upgrades.md`](../../interchange/drivetrain/junkyard-upgrades.md)

## AX-15 Manual Transmission

The AX-15 5-speed manual was used in XJ from mid-1988 (introduced mid-year) through 2001 with the 4.0L. Also used in YJ Wrangler.

**Compatible donors:**

| Donor | Years / Engine | Bellhousing notes |
|-------|---------------|-------------------|
| XJ Cherokee | 1989–1995 (4.0L) | Bellhousing matches 4.0L |
| YJ Wrangler | 1989–1995 (4.0L) | Same bellhousing as XJ 4.0L |
| YJ Wrangler | 1989–1995 (2.5L) | **Different bellhousing** — 2.5L and 4.0L bellhousings are not the same |
| Dodge Dakota | 1994+ V6 | Internals interchange but requires XJ bellhousing — Dakota bellhousing does not fit |

**Bellhousing shifts in the AX-15 production run:**
- 1989–1991: Smaller pilot tip (~19/32"), internal slave cylinder
- 1992+: Larger pilot tip (~3/4"), external slave (1994+)

When sourcing an AX-15 from a YJ or Dakota, the bellhousing from the donor does not carry over. Use the bellhousing from the XJ recipient vehicle or source a matching one.

## NP231 Transfer Case

The NP231 transfer case was used in XJ from 1987–2001 and is highly interchangeable across that range.

**Compatible donors:**

| Donor | Years | Notes |
|-------|-------|-------|
| XJ Cherokee | 1987–2001 | Highly interchangeable; most common donor |
| ZJ Grand Cherokee | 1993–1998 | NP231 variant; verify input shaft and linkage before swapping |
| MJ Comanche | 1987–1992 | Compatible |

**Slip yoke vs. fixed flange output:** Later NP231 cases (post-1996 in some configurations) may differ in output shaft design. Verify the output type (slip yoke or fixed flange) before purchasing a used case if an SYE is involved.

**Internal upgrade:** The Dodge truck NP231 uses a wider chain internally. Swapping the Dodge chain and sprockets into an XJ NP231 case is a documented durability upgrade — see [`../../interchange/drivetrain/junkyard-upgrades.md`](../../interchange/drivetrain/junkyard-upgrades.md).

For the NV241-OR (Rubicon 4:1 low range) swap from TJ Rubicon, see: [`../../interchange/drivetrain/junkyard-upgrades.md`](../../interchange/drivetrain/junkyard-upgrades.md)
