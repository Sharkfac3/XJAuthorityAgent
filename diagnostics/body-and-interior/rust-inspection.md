# Rust Inspection — Quick Reference

## Use this when
You want to assess an XJ for rust before buying, before lifting, or before planning a repair. This is a checklist-format companion to the full stock reference at [`vehicle/systems/body/rust-inspection.md`](../../vehicle/systems/body/rust-inspection.md).

---

## What you need
- Flashlight
- Flat-blade screwdriver (poke test)
- Mirror (optional, useful under the cargo floor)
- Gloves

---

## Checklist — work in this order

### 1. Rear frame rails and shackle mounts
**Location:** Under the rear of the vehicle, just ahead of the rear wheel, near the rear bumper

- [ ] Find the shackle hanger (U-shaped bracket where rear spring attaches)
- [ ] Poke the surrounding box section — does the screwdriver sink in?
- [ ] Rock the shackle hanger by hand — any movement?
- [ ] Shine flashlight up at the cargo floor from underneath — visible holes?

**If yes to any:** Major structural rust. See decision tree below.

---

### 2. Rocker panels
**Location:** Full-length sill running from front wheel opening to rear wheel opening

- [ ] Press firmly with thumb every 6–8 inches along the full outer length on both sides
- [ ] Poke any soft, bubbled, or separated areas
- [ ] From inside the cab, check the floor/sill junction under the carpet edge on both sides

**If soft spots or screwdriver penetrates:** Structural rust. See decision tree below.

---

### 3. Floor pans
**Location:** Inside the cab, under carpet

- [ ] Pull back or press on carpet — does the floor flex more than expected?
- [ ] Visible rust staining through or around the carpet?
- [ ] Musty or moldy smell when carpet is disturbed?
- [ ] Driver's floor and rear passenger floor, near the sills

**If holes or heavy flex:** Moderate structural concern; check rocker condition adjacent to any floor pan holes.

---

### 4. Firewall lower section
**Location:** Engine bay, passenger side, near the HVAC box; also inside at passenger footwell base

- [ ] Visible rust-through or large scale buildup at firewall base?
- [ ] Passenger footwell wet or stained at firewall?

**If yes:** Indicates long-term water intrusion; expect floor pan rust to be worse than visible.

---

### 5. Visual sweep
- [ ] Lower quarter panels and rear corners — heavy rust indicates moisture history
- [ ] Door bottoms — rust-through indicates standing water in doors
- [ ] Spare tire mount attachment points — move the carrier, check for loose mounting

---

## Decision tree

**Rear frame rails are rusted through or shackle hangers are loose:**
→ Major structural repair required (cut-and-weld new steel)
→ Vehicle cannot be safely lifted at factory rear jack points until repaired
→ Evaluate repair cost vs. vehicle value before proceeding

**Rockers have rust-through in multiple sections:**
→ Structural repair required; rocker replacement is significant labor
→ Also check B-pillar base for separation

**Floor pan holes with adjacent rocker rust:**
→ Compound repair; cost escalates significantly
→ Determine extent of both before committing to repair

**Firewall and floor pan rust, no rocker rust-through:**
→ Manageable; floor pan patches and firewall sealing are moderate repairs

**Surface rust and scale at several locations, no soft spots, no rust-through:**
→ Normal condition for an XJ of this age
→ Address water intrusion sources to prevent progression; see [`vehicle/systems/body/water-intrusion.md`](../../vehicle/systems/body/water-intrusion.md)
