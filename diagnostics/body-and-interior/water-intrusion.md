# Water Intrusion — Symptom Isolation

## Use this when
You have wet carpet, dripping inside the cabin, a wet headliner, or water staining on interior trim. This file helps isolate which entry point is responsible. Full stock leak-point reference: [`vehicle/systems/body/water-intrusion.md`](../../vehicle/systems/body/water-intrusion.md).

---

## Isolate by location first

### Wet driver's front floor

**Most likely:** Windshield seal failure at the lower driver's corner
- Water tracks down the A-pillar behind the dash and pools at the driver's footwell
- Check for water staining on the A-pillar trim or behind the dash

**Also possible:** Driver's door seal not seating; door drain holes clogged

**Rule out:** Heater core leak — coolant has a sweet smell and will leave a film on the windshield

---

### Wet passenger front floor

**Most likely:** Windshield seal failure at the lower passenger corner OR firewall grommet failure
- Windshield: water tracks down passenger A-pillar
- Firewall: water enters through deteriorated harness/HVAC grommets at the firewall base

**Also possible:** HVAC heater box drain clogged — condensation overflows into passenger footwell
- This presents as water after running the A/C or defrost, not just after rain

---

### Wet rear floor

**Most likely:** Tailgate upper hatch seal failure
- Water runs down the interior of the rear hatch opening
- Check for staining on the rear interior pillars and cargo trim panels

**Also possible:** Rear door seal failure; body seam cracks at the top of the tailgate opening

---

### Wet headliner or dripping near the sunroof (sunroof-equipped XJs only)

**Most likely:** Sunroof drain tube clogged or detached
- Rear drain tubes are more commonly blocked
- Water pools in the sunroof tray until it overflows into the headliner

**Confirm:** Pour a small amount of water into the sunroof channel and watch where it exits. It should exit at the lower body exterior. If it doesn't appear within a few seconds, the drain is blocked. If water drips inside instead, the tube is detached.

---

### Wet inside the door (visible when door panel is removed)

**Expected:** Some moisture inside door cavities is normal
**Concern:** Standing water or rust at the door bottom

**Cause:** Door drain holes at the bottom of the door cavity are clogged with debris
**Fix:** Clear drain holes with a pick or thin wire

**Secondary concern:** If the interior dam (the plastic sheet between door panel and door) is torn or missing, water that enters the door cavity will enter the cab

---

## After isolating the entry point

Once you know where water is getting in, the repair is targeted:
- Windshield seal → reseal or replace windshield
- Firewall grommets → replace/reseal grommets at the affected penetrations
- Tailgate hatch seal → replace upper hatch weatherstripping
- Sunroof drains → clear with compressed air or thin wire; if detached, reattach tube to fitting
- Door seals → replace weatherstripping on affected doors; clear drain holes

---

## Why this matters — structural consequence

Water that enters the cab pools under the carpet. On an XJ (unibody), pooled water causes floor pan rust, which is structurally adjacent to the rocker panels. Unaddressed water intrusion is the most common pathway to structural rust on an XJ.

After sealing leaks, inspect for rust damage already present. See [`diagnostics/body-and-interior/rust-inspection.md`](rust-inspection.md).
