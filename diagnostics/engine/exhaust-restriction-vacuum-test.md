# Exhaust Restriction — Vacuum Test

## Purpose
Diagnose a restricted exhaust (plugged catalytic converter or collapsed pipe) using a vacuum gauge on XJ and MJ engines.

---

## Tools
- Vacuum gauge with instruction booklet

---

## Procedure

### Step 1 — Baseline reading
1. Connect the vacuum gauge to an intake manifold vacuum source.
2. Start the engine and allow it to idle.
3. Record the idle vacuum reading.
   - Normal range: **17–21 inches of vacuum**

### Step 2 — Acceleration hold test
1. Throttle the engine up to **2,000–2,500 RPM** and hold for approximately 20 seconds.
2. The vacuum reading should stabilize to approximately the same value as idle.
   - If vacuum drops and keeps dropping under sustained RPM: suspect exhaust restriction.

### Step 3 — Snap throttle test
1. Quickly release the throttle from elevated RPM.
2. Vacuum should **spike approximately 5 inches higher** than the baseline idle reading for a moment, then quickly return to the idle baseline.
   - **Normal:** Sharp spike up, quick return.
   - **Restricted:** Reading stays elevated and comes down **slowly with jerky needle movement**.

---

## Interpreting results

| Behavior | Interpretation |
|----------|---------------|
| Stabilizes at idle vacuum under sustained RPM | Normal |
| Drops progressively under sustained RPM | Exhaust restriction likely |
| Snap-throttle spike returns slowly with jerky movement | Exhaust restriction confirmed |

A restricted catalytic converter is the most common cause on higher-mileage XJs.
