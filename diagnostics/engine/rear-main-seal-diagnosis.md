# Rear Main Seal Diagnosis

## Purpose
Correctly identify the source of an apparent rear main seal (RMS) oil leak before committing to RMS replacement.

## Why this matters
The RMS sits at the lowest point of a nose-up mounted engine. Oil from leaks anywhere above it flows down and pools at the RMS area, making every oil leak look like an RMS failure. Mechanics and previous owners commonly misdiagnose this. Verify the source yourself before authorizing or performing an RMS replacement.

---

## Check these first — in order

| Component | Why it can mimic an RMS leak |
|-----------|------------------------------|
| Valve cover gasket | Oil drains down the back of the block |
| Oil pressure sending unit | Located on the block; seeps and runs down |
| Oil filter adapter seals | Seep and track toward the rear |
| Distributor gasket | Oil weeps and travels down the back of the block |

Investigate and eliminate each of these before concluding the RMS is the source.

---

## Distributor sealing note
When reseating or reinstalling the distributor, use an O-ring (NAPA #727-2024) instead of a traditional flat gasket for improved sealing. See `work/setup-and-calibration/engine/renix-distributor-indexing.md` for distributor reinstallation procedure.
