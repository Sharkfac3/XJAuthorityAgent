# AW4 Fluid Condition

## Purpose
Assess AW4 transmission fluid condition, determine whether a service is overdue, and guide pan drop inspection for transmission health assessment.

## Symptom definition
Use this leaf when:
- Fluid pulled from the dipstick is dark, brown, or smells burnt
- Transmission is slipping or behaving erratically and fluid condition is unknown
- The vehicle has unknown service history and fluid baseline needs to be established
- A pan drop is planned for solenoid access and inspection is needed

---

## Fluid type
The AW4 requires **Dexron II or Dexron III** compatible fluid. Dexron III is the current standard.

**Do not use ATF+4.** ATF+4 is correct for the 42RE automatic used in the Grand Cherokee but is chemically incompatible with the AW4. Using ATF+4 in an AW4 will cause slip and shift quality degradation over time.

---

## Normal vs. degraded fluid

| Condition | Appearance | Smell | Action |
|-----------|-----------|-------|--------|
| Normal | Translucent red-pink | Faint petroleum smell | No action needed |
| Aged but serviceable | Slightly darker red | Mild, flat smell | Change if interval is due |
| Overdue | Dark brown, not translucent | Dull or stale smell | Change; inspect pan |
| Burnt | Black or very dark brown | Sharp burnt smell | Change; inspect pan for debris |
| Coolant contamination | Pink/milky or foamy | Unusual | Inspect radiator transmission cooler for internal leak |

Pull the dipstick on a warm engine (after a drive cycle), wipe on a white shop rag, and compare. A clean white rag makes the color easy to read.

---

## Pan drop procedure

Dropping the pan is the only way to inspect the filter, magnets, and pan debris. The AW4 has a drain plug, but the drain plug alone does not allow filter or magnet inspection.

### What to look for

**Magnets:** The pan has two magnets that collect metallic wear particles.
- Fine black powder on a magnet: normal ferrous accumulation over time
- Coarse metallic chips or flakes: abnormal wear; indicates clutch pack or gear damage
- Bright metal shavings: significant mechanical failure in progress

**Pan floor:**
- Light sludge: normal in a high-mileage transmission
- Clutch material (dark brown fibrous debris): clutch pack wear; indicates the slipping symptom has been ongoing
- Gear teeth fragments: major internal damage

**Filter:**
- No scheduled replacement interval on the AW4 unless the pan is already off
- Replace the filter screen and pan gasket any time the pan is removed
- A clogged filter can cause shift quality problems and solenoid erraticism by starving the hydraulic circuit

### Fluid capacity note
The drain plug alone drains roughly 3 quarts. Pan drop drains approximately 4 quarts. Total transmission capacity is approximately 8.5 quarts; the remainder stays in the torque converter and is not fully serviced by a simple pan drop. A staged fluid change (drain, refill, drive, drain again) gradually refreshes the full circuit without dropping the pan repeatedly.

---

## Fluid change interval
- Normal use: every 30,000 miles
- Severe use (frequent towing, off-road, stop-and-go): every 15,000 miles
- Unknown history: change immediately; pan drop recommended to assess internal condition

---

## Exit criteria

- **Fluid is dark or burnt + pan shows clutch debris:** significant internal wear; fluid change alone will not restore function; consult a transmission rebuild decision
- **Fluid is dark but pan is clean:** overdue service; change fluid and filter, recheck shift quality
- **Fluid is normal but shift quality complaint persists:** route to [`aw4-shift-quality.md`](aw4-shift-quality.md) for electrical and mechanical causes
