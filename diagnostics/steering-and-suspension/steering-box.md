# Steering Box

## Purpose
Fault-isolate XJ steering box problems: sector shaft slop, excessive steering wheel free play, and determining whether adjustment or replacement is the correct next step.

## Symptom definition
Use this leaf when:
- There is excessive free play (dead zone) at the steering wheel before the front wheels respond
- The steering wanders or requires constant correction, and other steering components (tie rod ends, drag link, track bar) have been inspected and found serviceable
- Steering wheel centering or on-center feel has degraded

## Do not use this leaf when
- Violent front-end oscillation occurs at highway speeds → [`death-wobble.md`](death-wobble.md) — the steering box is rarely the primary death wobble cause
- Free play is concentrated at a specific steering linkage connection (drag link end, pitman arm, etc.) → inspect that component before condemning the box

---

## Background: XJ steering box type

The XJ Cherokee uses a **recirculating ball steering gear** on all years. This is not a rack-and-pinion system. The gear converts steering wheel rotation into linear pitman arm movement via a ball nut that rides on a threaded worm shaft. Recirculating balls carry the load and reduce friction.

The gear has two adjustment points:
1. **Worm bearing preload** — set during assembly; rarely adjusted in the field
2. **Over-center (sector shaft) adjustment** — the primary in-service adjustment for free play at center

---

## Steering wheel free play: normal vs. excessive

Some free play at the steering wheel is normal — there should be a small dead zone at center. An excessive dead zone before the wheels respond indicates wear in the gear.

**Key distinction:** Is the free play present throughout the full steering range, or only at center?

- **Free play throughout the full range:** Indicates wear across the ball nut circuit, not just at the center mesh point. Over-center adjustment will not fix this.
- **Free play primarily at center:** The sector and ball nut mesh is worn at the high-use center position. Over-center adjustment may help.

---

## Over-center adjustment

The sector shaft adjustment screw tightens the mesh between the ball nut and the sector gear at the straight-ahead (over-center) position. Tightening it reduces free play at center but increases steering effort slightly.

**When it helps:** Free play that is significantly worse at center than at full lock positions. The gear is worn mainly at the most-used point.

**When it does not help:**
- Free play throughout the entire steering range (worn ball circuit, not just the sector mesh)
- A box that has already been overtightened — further tightening binds the gear
- A box where the sector shaft bearing or worm bearing is worn

**Critical caution:** Overtightening the adjuster screw is a common mistake. Running the adjuster too tight accelerates wear on the sector and ball nut. If the adjustment is tightened to remove free play and the box still feels loose, the box is worn beyond what adjustment can correct.

**In-vehicle adjustment is possible** but the manufacturer's procedure requires measuring over-center rotating torque with a torque wrench on the steering column stub shaft. Field adjustment without measurement is imprecise and risks overtightening.

---

## Replacement threshold

Replace the steering box when:
- Free play is present throughout the steering range (not only at center)
- Over-center adjustment at its proper limit does not reduce free play to an acceptable level
- The box has a history of repeated over-center adjustments tightened to the mechanical stop

High-mileage XJs commonly need steering box replacement as a part of a steering rebuild that also includes tie rod ends, drag link, and track bar. Replacing the box alone on a vehicle with worn linkage will not eliminate all steering play.

**Rebuilt vs. new:** Reman boxes are widely available and are the standard replacement path. A reman box from a reputable supplier is preferred over a new no-name unit. Confirm the replacement box is appropriate for the XJ's year and configuration (there are minor year-to-year differences).

---

## Exit criteria

- **Free play at center, adjustment makes it acceptable:** box serviceable; monitor
- **Free play throughout range or adjustment fails:** replace steering box; budget for linkage inspection at the same time
- **Box tightened to stop, still loose:** replace; the gear is mechanically worn past service limits
