# Lift Height and Clearance Planning

Lift requirements, suspension travel, and fender clearance planning for XJ Cherokee one-ton axle swaps (2005+ Super Duty Dana 60 front / Sterling 10.50 rear).

---

## Minimum lift height

One-ton axles are physically larger than any stock XJ axle. The housing diameter, differential pumpkin, and full-float hubs all require significantly more clearance between the axle and the XJ frame/body.

| Lift height | Viability | Notes |
|-------------|-----------|-------|
| Under 5" | Not viable | Housing contacts frame at full stuff. No room for suspension travel. |
| 5–6" | Marginal | Tight. Requires aggressive bump stops. Very limited travel. May work for dedicated trail rig with minimal street use. |
| 6–7" | Workable | Most common minimum for one-ton XJ builds. Adequate clearance with proper bump stop placement. |
| 7–8" | Ideal | Best balance of clearance, travel, and driveline angles. Most one-ton XJ builders land here. |
| 8"+ | Possible | Diminishing returns. Driveline angles become steep, CV joints work harder, center of gravity rises. Requires careful driveshaft and pinion angle planning. |

**Community consensus: 7–8" of lift for a one-ton XJ running 37–40" tires.**

---

## Frame clearance at full compression (stuff)

At full compression, the axle housing rises toward the XJ frame. The critical clearance points are:

### Front
- **Differential pumpkin to frame crossmember** — the Dana 60 center section is much larger than the Dana 30. Verify clearance at full stuff with the steering components attached.
- **Coil buckets (removed) and radius arm mounts (removed)** — if these are properly cut and ground flush, they are not a clearance issue. If cut poorly, stubs can contact the frame.
- **Steering linkage to frame** — high-steer arms and crossover tie rod must clear the frame at full compression and full lock simultaneously.

### Rear
- **Differential pumpkin to floor pan** — the Sterling center section hangs lower than any stock XJ rear axle. Verify it does not contact the XJ floor pan or gas tank skid at full stuff.
- **Full-float hubs to inner fender** — the hubs protrude further inboard than semi-float axles. Check clearance with the wheel and tire mounted.

---

## Bump stop placement

Bump stops are mandatory on a one-ton XJ build. They prevent the axle from contacting the frame at full compression.

**Guidelines:**
- Set bump stops to allow the maximum suspension travel that still maintains frame-to-axle clearance (typically 1–1.5" of clearance at the bump stop contact point)
- Front bump stops mount to the frame, contacting the axle tubes or spring perches
- Rear bump stops mount to the frame, contacting the spring perches or axle tubes
- Use progressive-rate bump stops (hockey puck style or hydraulic) to avoid harsh bottom-out
- Test clearance with the tires at full lock (front) at the bump stop — steering components must clear

## Fender and body clearance

### Fender trimming
With 37–40" tires on one-ton axles, the stock XJ fenders will not provide adequate clearance. Options:

| Approach | Clearance gained | Notes |
|----------|-----------------|-------|
| Fender trimming (cut and fold) | Moderate | Cut the lower fender lip and fold it up. Most common approach. Free. |
| Flat fenders (aftermarket) | Significant | Replace the stock fenders entirely with flat or tube-style fenders. Best clearance. |
| Inner fender trimming | Moderate | Cut the plastic inner fender liners for tire clearance at full lock/stuff. |
| Tube fenders | Maximum | Minimal fender coverage. Maximum clearance. Exposes engine bay to elements. |

### Tire-to-body contact points
Check clearance at these positions:
1. Full driver-side lock + full compression (front)
2. Full passenger-side lock + full compression (front)
3. Full droop (front and rear) — tire should not pull away from the body excessively
4. Full compression (rear) — tire to bed side or quarter panel

## Tire size vs lift height matrix

| Tire size | Minimum lift | Recommended lift | Fender work required |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| 35" | 5–6" | 6–7" | Moderate trimming |
| 37" | 6–7" | 7–8" | Significant trimming or flat fenders |
| 38–39" | 7–8" | 8" | Flat fenders or tube fenders recommended |
| 40" | 8"+ | 8"+ | Flat fenders or tube fenders required |

## Driveline angle limits

Higher lift = steeper angles at the transfer case outputs. This affects vibration and component life.

| Operating angle | Assessment |
|----------------|------------|
| Under 5° | Ideal. Smooth operation, long U-joint life. |
| 5–8° | Acceptable with double-cardan (CV) joints at the transfer case end. |
| 8–12° | Marginal. CV joints work hard. May develop vibration. Pinion angle becomes critical. |
| Over 12° | Not recommended. Premature CV joint and U-joint failure. Reconsider lift height or transfer case drop. |

**SYE + CV driveshafts are mandatory at 6"+ lift** — see [`../../../sourcing/interchange/axles/one-ton-driveshaft-specs.md`](../../../sourcing/interchange/axles/one-ton-driveshaft-specs.md)
