# Rear Spring Perch Welding — Sterling 10.50

Leaf spring perch placement, pinion angle setting, and welding procedures for the Sterling 10.50 rear axle under a Jeep Cherokee XJ.

---

## Why perch work is needed

The Sterling housing from the donor may have coil-spring mounts, link mounts, or leaf spring perches — but none of them will match XJ leaf spring geometry. New weld-on leaf spring perches must be placed for XJ leaf spring width and center-pin location.

## Perch specifications

### Dimensions
- **Width:** match XJ rear leaf spring pack width — approximately 2.5"
- **Perch type:** weld-on flat-pad perches designed for 2.5" leaf springs
- **Center pin hole:** must match the XJ leaf spring center pin diameter

### Spacing (center-to-center)
- Stock XJ rear spring pad spacing: approximately **42" center-to-center**
- Match this spacing on the Sterling housing
- If running a wider-than-stock track, adjust proportionally — but staying near 42" is standard

## Perch placement procedure

### 1. Establish axle centerline
- Measure from the center of the differential housing outward to each perch location
- Both sides must be equal — asymmetric placement shifts the rear axle off-center

### 2. Set fore-aft position (center pin location)
- The center pin determines where the axle sits relative to the XJ frame-mounted spring hangers and shackles
- The center pin must align so that the rear axle is centered in the wheelwell with equal spacing to the front of the fender opening and the rear
- Fore-aft position also sets the driveshaft angle to the transfer case

### 3. Set pinion angle

This is the most critical measurement for the rear axle.

**Pinion angle targets:**
- The rear pinion should point upward toward the transfer case rear output
- Target: **2–3 degrees downward** from the transfer case output angle (the driveshaft operating angle is the difference between the two)
- This creates a slight operating angle at the U-joints, which is needed for U-joint lubrication (zero angle causes premature wear)

**Measuring:**
- Use a digital inclinometer on the pinion yoke/flange face
- Compare to the inclinometer reading on the transfer case rear output flange
- The difference is the operating angle
- Target operating angle: 2–4 degrees total

**How pinion angle is set:**
- Rotating the spring perch forward or rearward on the tube before welding rotates the entire axle housing
- Moving the perch forward (toward the pinion) rotates the pinion upward
- Moving the perch rearward (toward the diff cover) rotates the pinion downward
- Small adjustments make a big difference — move in 1/8" increments and re-check

### 4. Tack and verify
- Tack-weld one perch in position
- Mock the axle under the XJ with leaf springs in place (on jack stands at ride height)
- Verify:
  - Axle is centered side-to-side under the body
  - Pinion angle is within 2–3 degrees of target
  - Springs sit flat on the perches
  - Driveshaft angle looks reasonable
- Tack the second perch and re-verify all measurements
- Make adjustments before final welding

### 5. Final weld
- Full-penetration weld around the entire perch-to-tube interface
- Weld both the inboard and outboard edges of each perch
- Allow slow cooling — no water quench
- Grind any weld splatter that interferes with spring seating

## Spring-over-axle (SOA) configuration

Like the front, one-ton XJ rear builds run spring-over-axle. The perch welds to the **top** of the tube. The U-bolts wrap over the spring and under the tube, clamping the assembly together.

## Common mistakes

- **Wrong pinion angle** — the most common error. Causes driveline vibration that may not be apparent until highway speed. Always verify with an inclinometer.
- **Perches not level** — a tilted perch causes the spring to load unevenly, which can crack a leaf or cause the axle to shift.
- **Unequal side-to-side distance** — the axle sits off-center, and the vehicle pulls or tracks crooked.
- **Inadequate weld preparation** — perch welds on paint, rust, or mill scale will fail. Clean to bare metal before placing perches.
