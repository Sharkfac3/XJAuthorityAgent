# Rear Axle Housing Preparation — Sterling 10.50

Housing narrowing, tube work, and axle shaft modification for installing a 2005+ Super Duty Sterling 10.50 rear axle under a Jeep Cherokee XJ.

For fitment dimensions and narrowing decisions, see: [`../../../sourcing/interchange/axles/super-duty-sterling-fitment.md`](../../../sourcing/interchange/axles/super-duty-sterling-fitment.md)

---

## Overview

The Sterling 10.50 SRW arrives from the donor approximately 7.5" wider than the stock XJ rear axle. Unlike the front Dana 60, the rear housing narrowing is simpler — no steering knuckles, no inner C geometry to worry about. It's a straight tube cut, shaft shortening, and re-weld operation.

## Step 1 — Remove unwanted brackets

Remove any donor-specific brackets from the housing tubes:
- Shock mounts (XJ mounts will be fabricated in a different position)
- Sway bar mounts
- ABS line brackets
- Any coil spring or link mounts (if the donor used a coil-spring rear suspension)

Cut and grind flush with the tube surface. Inspect tubes for heat damage after grinding.

## Step 2 — Narrowing the housing

### Target width
- Stock XJ rear: ~58.5" WMS-to-WMS
- Typical one-ton XJ target: **58–62" WMS**
- Many builders match the rear track width to the front (after Dana 60 narrowing) for consistent vehicle stance
- Consider tire width and fender clearance when choosing final width

### Tube cutting procedure
1. **Measure and mark** the desired tube length on each side, measuring from the center section outward
2. **Cut the tubes** at the marks — a band saw or pipe cutter gives the cleanest cut. Sawzall works but requires more cleanup.
3. **Preserve the tube ends** — the outer tube sections (where the bearing and seal surfaces are) must be re-attached after shortening. Keep them intact.
4. **Sleeve and re-weld** — slide the outer tube stubs (with bearing surfaces) into or over the shortened inner tubes. The sleeve joint must be tight-fitting. Weld fully around the circumference.
5. **Check alignment** — both tubes must be straight, parallel, and at equal length from the center section. Use a straightedge across both hub mounting surfaces.

### Key difference from front narrowing
The rear housing narrowing is geometrically simpler because:
- No inner C to re-orient (the center section is a standard pumpkin — tubes are pressed in straight)
- No steering knuckle geometry to preserve
- The cut is a simple tube shortening on both sides

## Step 3 — Axle shaft shortening

Full-float Sterling axle shafts must be shortened to match the narrowed housing.

### Process
1. Measure the required shaft length from the differential side gear to the hub flange face
2. Cut the shaft on the differential end (the splined end that engages the side gear)
3. Re-spline the cut end to match the side gear spline count (35-spline)
4. Heat-treat the re-splined area — cutting and re-splining removes the factory heat treatment

**This work must be done by a qualified machine shop or axle specialist.** Incorrect shaft length or poor re-splining will cause premature failure.

### Alternative: custom shafts
Order new shafts from a specialty manufacturer (Yukon, Superior, RCV) built to your exact narrowed length. More expensive but eliminates the risk of shop error on shortening. Recommended for builds running 40"+ tires or competing.

## Step 4 — Inspection before assembly

Before proceeding to spring perch welding:
- Verify all welds are fully penetrated and ground smooth
- Check tube straightness with a long straightedge
- Verify both sides are equal length from center section
- Inspect hub assemblies (spindle nuts, lock washers, bearings) — replace any worn components
- Flush the differential housing interior to remove metal debris from cutting
- Inspect the ring-and-pinion for wear, chipping, or damage (replace if the gears will be changed anyway)
