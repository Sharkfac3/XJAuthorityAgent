# Front Spring Perch Relocation — Dana 60

Removing Super Duty coil spring buckets and welding XJ leaf spring perches to the Dana 60 front housing.

---

## Why this is needed

The Super Duty Dana 60 is a coil-spring axle. The XJ uses leaf springs. The coil buckets must be removed (see [`housing-prep.md`](housing-prep.md)) and replaced with weld-on leaf spring perches positioned for XJ leaf spring geometry.

## Perch specifications

### Perch dimensions
- **Width:** match XJ leaf spring pack width — stock XJ front leaf is approximately 2.5" wide
- **Perch pad width:** use perches designed for 2.5" springs (commonly available as weld-on perches from off-road fabrication suppliers)
- **Center pin hole:** the perch must have a center pin hole that matches the XJ leaf spring center pin

### Perch spacing (center-to-center)
- Stock XJ front spring pad spacing: approximately **32" center-to-center**
- Match this spacing on the Dana 60 housing to maintain correct spring geometry
- If running a wider track (narrowed housing wider than stock XJ), adjust perch spacing proportionally — but keeping it close to 32" is standard practice

## Perch placement procedure

### 1. Establish the axle centerline
- The perches must be placed symmetrically about the housing centerline
- Measure from the center of the differential housing outward to each perch location
- Equal distance on both sides is critical — asymmetric placement shifts the axle under the XJ

### 2. Set the fore-aft position (center pin location)
- The center pin determines where the axle sits relative to the spring eye mounts on the XJ frame
- The center pin should align so that the axle is centered under the XJ (equal wheelbase on both sides)
- The fore-aft position also affects the pinion angle — moving the perch forward or rearward rotates the axle housing

### 3. Set pinion angle
- **Target pinion angle:** the front pinion should point upward toward the transfer case front output at an angle that creates 2–4° of operating angle at the front driveshaft U-joint
- Too much angle = vibration and premature U-joint wear
- Too little angle = binding at full compression
- The pinion angle is set by rotating the perch fore-aft on the tube before tack-welding

### 4. Tack and verify
- Tack-weld one perch in position
- Set the axle under the XJ (on jack stands) with the leaf springs mocked up
- Verify:
  - Axle is centered side-to-side
  - Wheelbase is equal on both sides
  - Pinion angle is within target range
  - Springs sit flat on the perches without rocking
- Tack the second perch and re-verify
- Adjust as needed before final welding

### 5. Final weld
- Full-penetration weld around the entire perch-to-tube interface
- Weld on both sides of the perch (inboard and outboard)
- Let the housing cool slowly — do not quench with water (risks tube warpage)
- Grind any weld splatter that could interfere with spring seating

## Spring-over-axle (SOA) note

One-ton XJ builds typically run spring-over-axle (SOA) — the leaf spring sits on top of the axle tube. This is the default assumption for perch placement on the Dana 60. Spring-under-axle is not practical with a housing this large at typical XJ lift heights.

If running SOA, the perch welds to the **top** of the tube.

## Common mistakes

- **Perches not level** — if the perch pad is not flat and level on the tube, the spring will rock and the axle will shift under load. Use a machinist's level or flat bar to verify.
- **Unequal side-to-side distance** — causes the axle to sit off-center, pulling the XJ to one side.
- **Wrong pinion angle** — causes driveline vibration. Verify angle with an inclinometer before final welding.
- **Welding over paint or rust** — perch welds must be on clean bare metal. Wire wheel or grind the tube surface before placing the perch.
