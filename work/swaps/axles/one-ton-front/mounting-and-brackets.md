# Front Mounting and Brackets — Dana 60

Physical mounting, shock placement, and bump stop configuration for a 2005+ Super Duty Dana 60 front axle under a Jeep Cherokee XJ.

---

## Spring-over-axle (SOA)

One-ton XJ front builds run spring-over-axle — the leaf spring sits on top of the axle tube, and the axle hangs below the spring. This is the only practical configuration for a housing this large at XJ lift heights.

### Spring-to-axle connection
- Leaf spring sits on the weld-on spring perch (see [`spring-perch-relocation.md`](spring-perch-relocation.md))
- U-bolts wrap over the spring and under the axle tube, clamping them together
- U-bolt plate sits under the axle tube — this plate also serves as the lower shock mount in many configurations
- **U-bolt sizing:** use u-bolts sized for the Dana 60's 3.5" tube OD. Stock XJ u-bolts are too small.

### Spring pads
- Weld-on spring perches should have a flat pad surface that matches the leaf spring width (~2.5")
- Center pin hole aligns with the leaf spring center pin to prevent spring walk
- Anti-friction pads (polyurethane or rubber) between the spring and perch reduce noise and allow minor articulation

## Shock mount fabrication

The Dana 60 has no stock shock mounts compatible with XJ geometry. Both upper and lower mounts must be fabricated.

### Lower shock mounts
- **Most common:** weld-on shock tabs on the u-bolt plate (below the axle tube)
- Alternative: weld-on tabs directly on the axle tube (less common, requires careful placement to avoid spring interference)
- Mount orientation: shocks should angle slightly inward from bottom to top, matching the angle to the frame-mounted upper mounts

### Upper shock mounts
- Mount to the XJ frame rail in approximately the stock location
- If the stock XJ upper shock mounts are too low for the new suspension geometry, fabricate new mounts higher on the frame or use frame-mounted shock towers
- The upper mount position determines shock travel and effective damping range

### Shock selection
- **Travel requirement:** measure the distance between the upper and lower mounts at full droop and full compression. The shock must accommodate this range without bottoming out or over-extending.
- **Valving:** one-ton axles with 37–40" tires are significantly heavier than stock. Shocks must be valved for the increased unsprung weight. Stock XJ shocks will be far too soft.
- **Recommendations:** adjustable reservoir shocks (Fox, King, Bilstein 7100) for trail-focused builds. Heavy-duty monotube shocks (Bilstein 5100, Rancho 9000) for street/trail dual-use.

## Bump stop placement

### Purpose
Prevent the Dana 60 housing from contacting the XJ frame at full compression. The Dana 60 center section and steering components are significantly larger than the stock Dana 30 — frame contact will cause damage.

### Placement guidelines
- Mount bump stops to the XJ frame, positioned to contact the axle tube or spring perch at the desired maximum compression point
- Allow 1–1.5" of clearance between the housing/steering components and the frame at the bump stop contact point
- Use progressive-rate bump stops to avoid harsh bottoming — hockey-puck style rubber or hydraulic bump stops

### Critical clearance check
At the bump stop contact point, verify clearance at:
- Center section (differential pumpkin) to frame crossmember
- High-steer arms and tie rod to frame
- Brake calipers to frame or inner fender
- Full steering lock + full compression (worst case for front clearance)

## Radius arm mount removal

The Super Duty Dana 60 has radius arm mounting brackets welded to the bottom of the axle tubes. These are removed during housing preparation (see [`housing-prep.md`](housing-prep.md)). After removal:
- Verify the surfaces are ground flush — any protruding stubs will interfere with u-bolt plates or catch on trail obstacles
- The area where the radius arm mounts were is now available for lower shock mount tabs or traction device brackets if needed

## Sway bar (optional)

Most one-ton XJ trail builds delete the front sway bar for maximum articulation. If a sway bar is desired for on-road stability:
- Stock XJ sway bar and mounts will not work with the Dana 60 at the new ride height
- Aftermarket sway bar links (adjustable length) can connect a frame-mounted bar to the axle
- Quick-disconnect sway bar links allow easy removal for trail use
