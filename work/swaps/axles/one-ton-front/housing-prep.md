# Front Axle Housing Preparation — Dana 60

Housing narrowing, cleanup, and preparation procedures for installing a 2005+ Super Duty Dana 60 front axle under a Jeep Cherokee XJ.

For fitment dimensions and narrowing decisions, see: [`../../../sourcing/interchange/axles/super-duty-dana-60-fitment.md`](../../../sourcing/interchange/axles/super-duty-dana-60-fitment.md)

---

## Overview

The Super Duty Dana 60 arrives from the donor approximately 14" wider than needed for an XJ. The housing also has coil spring buckets and radius arm mounts that must be removed. This file covers preparing the bare housing for XJ installation.

## Step 1 — Remove unwanted brackets

### Coil spring buckets
- Located on top of the axle tubes, one per side
- Cut with a sawzall or cutting wheel as close to the tube as possible
- Grind the weld remnants flush with the tube surface
- Inspect the tube for heat damage or gouges after grinding

### Radius arm mounts
- Located on the bottom of the axle tubes, one per side
- Heavy cast brackets — cut with a sawzall, then grind flush
- These are the largest brackets to remove — take care not to gouge the tube wall

### Other donor-specific brackets
- Sway bar mounts, ABS line brackets, and any other Super Duty suspension brackets on the housing
- Remove and grind flush

**After all brackets are removed**, the housing should be clean tubes with the center section (inner C and differential) intact.

## Step 2 — Narrowing the housing

### Target width
- Stock XJ Dana 30 front: ~57.2" WMS-to-WMS
- Typical one-ton XJ target: **60–64" WMS** depending on tire width and desired track width
- Wider track (62–64") provides better stability but requires more fender clearance
- Narrower track (60–62") fits more easily but may reduce tire clearance at full lock

### Cut-and-turn method
This is the most common narrowing approach:

1. **Measure and mark** the desired tube length on each side, measuring from the center section outward
2. **Cut the outer tube** at the mark — this is where the knuckle end (outer stub) will be re-attached
3. **Turn the inner C** — the inner C housing (the cast center section where the tubes press in) is cut on both sides, the tubes are shortened, and the inner C is re-welded to the shortened tubes
4. **Re-install the outer tube stubs** — the knuckle-end tube sections are sleeved over or into the shortened inner tubes and welded
5. **Check alignment** — the tubes must be straight and parallel. Use a straightedge across both knuckle mounting surfaces. Any misalignment causes tire wear and handling problems.

### Critical measurements during narrowing
- Equal length on both sides — measure from center section to knuckle mounting surface
- Tube straightness — check with a long straightedge or laser
- Pinion centerline — the pinion must remain centered in the housing (it shifts if one side is cut more than the other)

### Truss kits
Aftermarket truss kits reinforce the narrowed housing:
- Weld-on trusses bridge the inner C to the tubes, adding significant strength
- Some kits include pre-cut tubes at common XJ widths — eliminating the cut-and-turn step
- Recommended for any build running 37"+ tires and wheeling hard

## Step 3 — Axle shaft modification

After narrowing, the stock Super Duty axle shafts are too long.

**Options:**
- **Shorten the shafts** — a machine shop cuts and re-splines the inner end of each shaft. Must be done precisely — the shaft length must match the narrowed housing exactly.
- **Custom axle shafts** — order new shafts from a specialty shop (RCV, Yukon, Superior) built to your narrowed housing dimensions. More expensive but guaranteed to be correct.
- **Re-use one stock shaft** — if the narrowing is asymmetric (one side cut more than the other), one shaft may still be the correct length. Rare but possible.

## Step 4 — Inspection before assembly

Before proceeding to spring perch welding and assembly:
- Verify all welds are fully penetrated and ground smooth
- Check tube straightness one final time
- Inspect ball joints in the knuckles — replace if worn (much easier now than after installation)
- Inspect unit bearings — replace if any play or roughness is detected
- Clean the differential housing interior — flush any metal debris from cutting and grinding
