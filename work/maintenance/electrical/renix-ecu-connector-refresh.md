# Renix ECU Connector Refresh

## Purpose
Clean and tighten the ECU harness connectors on Renix-era (1987–1990) XJ and MJ vehicles to eliminate connection-related faults.

## Why this matters
The ECU sits under the dash and its connectors accumulate oxidation over decades. Loose female receptacles cause intermittent contact with the ECU pins — producing faults that mimic sensor failures or ECU failure. This is a low-effort step that should be done before condemning an ECU.

---

## ECU location
Under the dash, to the right of the steering column, mounted to a black bracket. The bracket attaches to the underside of the dash with two 10mm nuts.

---

## Procedure

### Tools
- Ratcheting wrench or socket (10mm)
- Small pick or dental tool
- Electronics contact cleaner (Hosa DeoxIT or equivalent)

### Steps
1. Remove the two 10mm nuts holding the ECU bracket to the underside of the dash. A ratcheting wrench works best in this location.
2. Lower the ECU from the bracket.
3. Unplug both harness connectors from the ECU.
4. Visually inspect all pins and connectors for corrosion, bent pins, or retracted terminals.
5. Liberally spray both harness connector ends and the ECU pin area with electronics cleaner.
6. Using a small pick or dental tool, gently tweak each female receptacle in the harness plugs to ensure tighter contact with the ECU pins.
7. Plug both harness connectors back onto the ECU.
8. Reinstall the ECU bracket with the two 10mm nuts.

---

## When to perform
- At vehicle acquisition
- Before diagnosing any intermittent electrical or driveability fault
- Before replacing an ECU suspected of failure
