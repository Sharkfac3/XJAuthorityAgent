# Renix Connector and Relay Refresh

## Purpose
Restore reliable contact across all engine bay electrical connections on Renix-era (1987–1990) XJ and MJ vehicles.

## Why this matters
Renix engine bay connectors accumulate corrosion and oxidation over decades. Terminals can retract into plastic housings without obvious visual signs. Relay receptacle contacts lose conductivity. These issues cause intermittent faults that mimic failed sensors or modules. This procedure should be performed on every Renix vehicle at acquisition or any time electrical reliability is in question.

---

## General engine bay connector refresh

### Procedure
1. Unplug every electrical connection in the engine bay.
2. Spray each connector and receptacle with electronics contact cleaner (Hosa DeoxIT or equivalent).
3. Visually inspect each terminal for retraction into the plastic housing — push any retracted terminals back to full seat.
4. Reconnect all connectors.

---

## Critical connectors

### 10-pin front lighting connector
- **Location:** In front of the air cleaner, behind the left headlight assembly.
- This connector is easy to overlook and is a common source of front lighting faults.
- Clean and inspect per the general procedure above.

### Ballast resistor connectors
- **Location:** Near the air cleaner housing.
- Ensure connectors are clean and seated tight.

---

## Relay maintenance

### Procedure
1. Remove all relays from the engine bay.
2. Wire-brush each relay terminal until shiny.
3. Spray each relay receptacle with contact cleaner.
4. Reinstall all relays.

---

## When to perform
- At vehicle acquisition (any Renix-era purchase)
- Before diagnosing intermittent electrical faults
- As part of a full electrical refresh alongside ground restoration
