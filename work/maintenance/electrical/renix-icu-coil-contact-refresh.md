# Renix ICU/Coil Contact Refresh

## Purpose
Restore reliable contact between the ignition coil and the ICU (Ignition Control Unit) on Renix-era (1987–1990) XJ and MJ vehicles.

## Why this matters
The spring-loaded contacts between the coil and ICU corrode and lose tension over time. This causes complete or intermittent no-start conditions that can be mistaken for a failed coil, ICM, or CPS. Perform this as a maintenance precaution at least once in the vehicle's life, and before diagnosing any ignition no-start.

---

## Coil-to-ICU contact procedure

### Tools
- T20 Torx driver
- Fine sandpaper
- Electronics contact cleaner (Hosa DeoxIT or equivalent)
- Needle-nose pliers
- OxGard

### Steps
1. Remove the two T20 Torx bolts holding the coil to the ICU.
2. Lift the coil away from the ICU to expose the two pins and two springy contact pieces.
3. Clean both the pins and the spring contacts with electronics cleaner and fine sandpaper.
4. Use needle-nose pliers to gently squeeze the springy contacts closer together — restoring contact tension.
5. Apply OxGard to the contacts.
6. Reinstall the coil and tighten the two T20 Torx bolts.

---

## ICU connector procedure

1. Unplug the ICU connectors.
2. Inspect each pin — verify none have retracted into the connector housing.
3. Spray out the connector and the ICU receptacle with electronics cleaner.
4. Reconnect.

---

## When to perform
- At vehicle acquisition
- Before diagnosing any ignition no-start or intermittent no-start
- As part of a full Renix electrical refresh
