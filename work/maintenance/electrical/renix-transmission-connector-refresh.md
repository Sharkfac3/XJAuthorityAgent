# Renix Transmission Connector Refresh

## Purpose
Clean and inspect the transmission and TCU connectors on Renix-era (1987–1990) XJ and MJ vehicles to eliminate connection faults in the transmission control circuit.

## Why this matters
These connectors carry signals between the TPS, TCU, NSS, speed sensor, and transmission solenoids. Corrosion or poor contact here causes shift quality issues, erratic behavior, or transmission faults that mimic component failures.

---

## Transmission connectors (engine bay)

### Location
Two large connectors near the transmission dipstick tube:
- **Black connector** — Neutral Safety Switch (NSS)
- **Gray connector** — transmission itself

### Procedure
1. Unplug both connectors.
2. Visually inspect all pins for corrosion or bending.
3. Spray each connector and receptacle with electronics contact cleaner.
4. Reconnect both connectors.

---

## TCU connector and fuse (under dash)

### Location
Under the glovebox area.

### Procedure
1. Reach under the glovebox and unplug the TCU connector.
2. Spray out the connector and the TCU receptacle with electronics contact cleaner.
3. Locate the TCU fuse in the same area.
4. Remove the fuse and clean its receptacle.
5. Remove any corrosion from the fuse itself.
6. Reinstall the fuse and reconnect the TCU connector.

---

## When to perform
- At vehicle acquisition
- Before diagnosing any shift quality, NSS, or transmission solenoid fault
- As part of a full Renix electrical refresh
