# Cooling System Refresh Procedure

## Purpose
Provide a narrow, baseline maintenance sequence for refreshing an aging stock or near-stock XJ cooling system.

## Scope
This is a catch-up maintenance workflow, not a deep repair manual. It assumes the goal is to renew common wear items and restore a known baseline.

## Typical refresh scope
A practical refresh usually includes some combination of:
- draining and refilling coolant
- inspecting or renewing upper and lower hoses
- inspecting heater hoses and small recovery/overflow hoses
- checking the cap and bottle condition
- checking belt condition and tension where applicable
- checking fan clutch, fan blade, shroud presence, and electric-fan operation
- cleaning external debris from the radiator and condenser face

## Triage before disassembly
Route the job out of maintenance and into diagnostics or repairs if you find:
- obvious external leakage from radiator seams, water pump, heater core area, or freeze-plug area
- recurring overheating with no clear maintenance explanation
- coolant contamination that suggests a larger engine problem
- damaged wiring, relays, or connectors at the fan-control side

## Baseline refresh sequence
1. Let the system cool fully before opening it.
2. Inspect coolant condition, hose age, clamp style, bottle condition, and cap sealing surfaces.
3. Inspect the radiator/condenser face for packed debris and airflow blockage.
4. Inspect fan hardware: missing shroud, weak clutch behavior, damaged blades, or non-working auxiliary electric fan.
5. Drain coolant and refill with the correct coolant strategy for the vehicle's current service baseline.
6. Renew aged hoses, clamps, and obvious low-cost wear items that would undermine the refresh.
7. Recheck belt condition and accessory-drive health if fan and pump speed depend on it.
8. Run the vehicle to operating temperature, confirm circulation and heater output, and verify there are no leaks.

## What this procedure intentionally does not settle
- exact coolant chemistry policy for every production year
- every radiator family or hose-routing variation
- thermostat, pump, or radiator replacement detail when those parts are already confirmed failed
