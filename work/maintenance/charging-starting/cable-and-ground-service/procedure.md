# Cable And Ground Service Procedure

## Purpose
Provide a narrow maintenance workflow for restoring battery-cable and ground-path health on an XJ.

## Scope
This procedure is for preventive service and baseline cleanup. It is not a full no-crank or full charging-system diagnostic tree.

## Typical service scope
A practical cable and ground service usually includes:
- disconnecting and cleaning battery terminals
- inspecting terminal fit and clamp damage
- inspecting positive and negative cable condition end-to-end
- inspecting block, chassis, and body ground attachment points
- correcting loose, corroded, painted, or oil-contaminated ground surfaces
- checking belt condition if charging performance complaints exist

## Service sequence
1. Record the symptom history so you can tell later whether maintenance solved it or only exposed a deeper fault.
2. Disconnect the battery safely.
3. Inspect both battery terminals for corrosion, distortion, or evidence of overheating.
4. Follow the major positive and negative cables to each end and inspect for stiffness, swelling, broken strands, or hidden corrosion.
5. Clean and tighten the primary battery-to-engine and battery-to-chassis ground points.
6. Clean and tighten the starter and alternator feed connections if they are part of the accessible service scope.
7. Reassemble, confirm secure routing away from exhaust and sharp edges, and verify battery hold-down condition.
8. Start the vehicle and recheck for hot terminals, voltage-drop symptoms, and charging complaints.

## Route out of maintenance when
Move into diagnostics or repair if you find:
- repeated low charging voltage after cable and ground service
- severe voltage drop under load
- damaged alternator or starter connections that need component repair
- melted connectors or failed fusible-link style sections that are no longer simple maintenance
