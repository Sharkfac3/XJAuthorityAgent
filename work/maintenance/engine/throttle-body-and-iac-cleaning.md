# Throttle Body and IAC Cleaning

## Purpose
Clean the Idle Air Control (IAC) valve and throttle body on XJ and MJ vehicles to restore idle quality and prevent carbon-related stalling.

## Applies to
All XJ/MJ model years. IAC mounting location varies by year:
- **1987–1990 (Renix):** IAC mounted on the front of the throttle body
- **1991+:** IAC mounted on the back of the throttle body

## When to perform
- As part of a normal tune-up
- Whenever idle quality is poor or stalling is present
- At vehicle acquisition as preventive maintenance

---

## IAC overview

The IAC is a stepper motor with a pintle-shaped plunger that controls airflow through the idle air passage:
- **Plunger retracted** → more air → higher idle speed
- **Plunger extended** → less air → lower idle speed

Carbon buildup on the pintle and seating area restricts movement and airflow, degrading idle quality over time.

---

## Tools and materials
- Torx driver
- Throttle body cleaner (not carburetor cleaner — throttle body cleaner is less harsh, safe for throttle body coatings and oxygen sensors)
- Rag
- Toothbrush and/or Q-tips
- New throttle body-to-manifold gasket (if removing the throttle body on 1991+)

---

## Procedure

### Step 1 — Remove air intake components
Remove the air filter cover, associated hoses, and the rubber boot running from the air filter cover to the throttle body.

### Step 2 — Remove the IAC
Remove the IAC using a Torx driver (2 bolts; one can be difficult to access).
- **1991 and later:** It may be easier to remove the entire throttle body. Use a new throttle body-to-manifold gasket on reinstallation.

### Step 3 — Extract the IAC
Gently wiggle the IAC out of the throttle body. The gasket/O-ring can be reused if undamaged.

### Step 4 — Clean the IAC
Spray the IAC with throttle body cleaner. Use the cleaner with a rag, toothbrush, and Q-tips to remove all carbon deposits.

**Critical:** Do not twist or pull on the pintle that protrudes from the IAC — it is fragile and easily damaged.

### Step 5 — Clean the IAC seating area
Thoroughly spray and flush the IAC seating bore in the throttle body with throttle body cleaner.

### Step 6 — Clean the throttle body
While disassembled, clean:
- The entire throttle body bore
- The butterfly valve and its edges
- All associated linkage

### Step 7 — Reinstall
Reinstall the IAC with its gasket/O-ring. Reinstall the air intake components.
