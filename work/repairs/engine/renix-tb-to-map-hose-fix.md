# Renix Throttle Body to MAP Sensor Hose Fix

## Purpose
Permanently repair the throttle body MAP sensor vacuum connection on Renix-era (1987–1990) XJ and MJ vehicles using a tapped fitting and new plastic tubing — replacing a discontinued OEM part.

## Problem
The Renix throttle body has a proprietary vacuum connection on its side where the MAP supply line originates. This fitting is no longer available as a replacement part. The hose and connector are prone to cracking, melting, and loosening. A failed MAP vacuum line causes hard starting and severe driveability problems — an incorrect MAP signal produces an excessively rich fuel mixture and can prevent starting entirely.

---

## Parts (NAPA)

| Part | NAPA number |
|------|-------------|
| Throttle body gasket | FPG 60742 |
| Vacuum fitting (1/8″ NPT) | 05703-B102 |
| Vacuum elbows (qty 2) | CRB2670 |
| Plastic tubing | CRB2672 |
| Thread sealer | Permatex #2 |

**Additional:** 1/8″ NPT tap, tap driver, oiler, carburetor cleaner

---

## Procedure

### Step 1 — Remove the throttle body
Remove the throttle body and bring it to a workbench for controlled work.

This is an ideal time to clean the throttle body and IAC: `work/maintenance/engine/throttle-body-and-iac-cleaning.md`

### Step 2 — Tap the lower hole
Using an oiled 1/8″ NPT tap, carefully thread the **lower** of the two holes on the throttle body side where the old fitting was plugged in.

- Use pipe threads — do not go too deep.
- The **upper hole does not pass all the way through** the throttle body — it only held the original vacuum tube in position. Do not tap it.

### Step 3 — Clean the hole
Flush the tapped hole thoroughly with carburetor cleaner. Inspect for any remaining metal cuttings.

### Step 4 — Install the vacuum fitting
Apply a small amount of Permatex #2 thread sealer to the threads of the vacuum fitting (05703-B102) only — not into the hole. Thread in until snug.

### Step 5 — Install elbows
Install vacuum elbows (CRB2670) on both the new throttle body fitting and the MAP sensor, angling them toward each other.

### Step 6 — Cut and route the tube
Cut a length of plastic tubing (CRB2672) approximately **13 inches** to fit between the two elbows. Leave enough slack to accommodate engine movement. Route carefully to avoid contact with moving or hot components.

### Step 7 — Reinstall the throttle body
Use a new throttle body gasket (FPG 60742). Reinstall and reconnect all vacuum and electrical connections.

---

## Special cases

### MAP already connected to intake manifold
On some vehicles the MAP sensor connects to the forward intake manifold rather than the throttle body. In this case, simply **cap the lower throttle body hole** after tapping — the repair proceeds identically except no new tube is needed from the throttle body.

### Alternative tubing
3/16″ brake line has been used successfully as a substitute for the plastic tubing.

---

## Post-repair verification
If a rough idle or black smoke develops after any throttle body work, spray carburetor cleaner around the MAP sensor vacuum line and intake manifold to check for vacuum leaks before diagnosing further.
