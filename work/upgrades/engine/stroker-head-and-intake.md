# Stroker Head, Intake, Cam, and Exhaust — Jeep 4.0L

A stroked 4.0L increases displacement and torque demand. The stock breathing package (head, intake, cam, exhaust) was designed for 242ci. At 276–284ci those components become the restriction. This page covers what to do about each one.

---

## Cylinder head

The stock 4.0L HO head is a cast iron unit with 1.91″ intake / 1.50″ exhaust valves. It flows well enough for a mild street stroker at stock compression, but its port velocity drops off at higher displacement and RPM.

**Port and polish (recommended for any stroker build):** A competent port and polish on the HO head removes casting flash, smooths the port transitions, and improves flow at no cost to low-end torque. This is the most cost-effective head work for a street/trail stroker.

**Head casting selection:** The 0630 casting (1996–1999 XJ) and the TUPY-marked 0331 casting (mid-2001+) are the preferred HO heads. Avoid the early 0331 casting (2000–mid-2001) — it is prone to cracking between cylinders 3–4. See `vehicle/era2/ho-engine.md` for full head casting details and cross-era installation notes.

**Aftermarket aluminum head:** Edelbrock and Comp Cams offer an aluminum Performer head for the 4.0L as a package (sold through Summit Racing). It reduces weight, improves flow, and uses a fast-burn combustion chamber. It is designed for use with a 1991–2006 intake manifold and requires an appropriate header. This is a meaningful upgrade for a power-oriented stroker build but adds significant cost.

## Intake manifold

The stock HO intake evolved during its production run. The later versions are real upgrades:

| Manifold | Years | Notes |
|----------|-------|-------|
| Straight-runner | 1991–1998 | Stock, functional, not ideal |
| Curved-runner ("good intake") | 1999–2000 | Revised runner geometry, noticeably better flow. Bolt-on upgrade. |
| 2001 XJ casting | 2001 | Largest stock casting. Best stock option. |

The 1999–2000 curved-runner intake is a well-documented bolt-on upgrade on any Group A HO engine. It has been referenced across NAXJA and JeepStrokers as worth the swap on a stroked engine.

## Camshaft

The stock HO cam is a reasonable starting point for a mild street stroker. Stroker engines can benefit from more duration to move the larger air/fuel charge through the longer piston stroke, but the gains depend heavily on how the rest of the engine is built.

**Comp Cams Xtreme 4×4 line** has documented Jeep 4.0L-specific grinds:

| Part Number | Years | Notes |
|-------------|-------|-------|
| 68-501-5 | 1964–1998 | Stock head, EFI. 197°/201° duration. |
| 68-301-5 | 1964–1998 | Aluminum head, EFI. |
| 68-401-5 | 1964–1998 | Aluminum head, carbureted. |
| 163-201-5 | 1999–2006 | Stock head and valve springs, EFI. 205°/213° duration. |
| 163-301-5 | 1999–2006 | Edelbrock aluminum head, EFI. |

All are hydraulic flat-tappet grinds matched to the 4.0L's stamped steel rocker arms. Comp also offers matching ovate wire valve springs (PN 983J-KIT) for lifts up to 0.450″ — required with any of the higher-lift Xtreme 4×4 grinds.

**Cam profile selection:** A trail and low-speed crawling build favors more duration overlap and low-end torque. A street/highway build typically runs a milder profile. If running an aftermarket ECU, the VE table can be tuned to match the cam's power band. On a stock ECU, aggressive cam profiles require more care with idle quality.

## Exhaust

The stock cast iron exhaust manifold is restrictive and is a limiting factor on any 4.0L build, stroked or not. A quality header is one of the highest-return upgrades on this engine.

Header options with documented Jeep 4.0L community use:
- **Banks Power** — long-tube header kits for XJ
- **Flowmaster** — headers for the 4.0L application
- **Clifford Performance** — Clifford has historically offered headers and intake combinations for AMC inline-six applications including the 4.0L. Verify current availability directly with the vendor.

A long-tube header paired with a quality cat-back exhaust system is the full package for a stroker build that is making real power.

---

## Summary: priority order for a street stroker build

1. **Header** — highest return on any 4.0L, stroked or not
2. **Port and polish the head** — cheap, effective, no downside
3. **1999+ curved-runner intake** — bolt-on, meaningful flow improvement
4. **Comp Cams Xtreme 4×4 grind** — matched to the displacement and intended use
5. **Aftermarket aluminum head** — high cost, high reward for a dedicated power build
