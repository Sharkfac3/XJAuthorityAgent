# Renix Valve Cover CCV Mod

## Purpose
Reduce excessive oil carryover into the air cleaner box on early Renix and later 4.0L engines by modifying the valve cover fluted tubes.

## Problem
Many early Renix 4.0L engines (and some later ones) push excessive oil through the crankcase ventilation (CCV) system into the air cleaner box. The factory fluted tubes inside the valve cover are too long, allowing oil mist to be pulled into the intake before it can drain back.

---

## Tools and materials
- Screwdriver (to remove fluted tube screws)
- Saw or cutting tool (to shorten tubes)
- Drill with 1/2″ bit
- File or sandpaper (deburring)
- Solvent (cleaning)
- Felpro valve cover gasket (replacement)

---

## Procedure

### Step 1 — Remove the valve cover
Remove the valve cover and invert it.

**Gasket note:** The factory gaskets on the fluted tube bases will likely crumble when removed. They do not need to be replaced — the tubes can go back in without gaskets.

### Step 2 — Remove the fluted tubes
Remove the three screws securing each fluted tube. Remove both tubes.

### Step 3 — Shorten the tubes
Cut **1 inch off** the bottom of each fluted tube.

### Step 4 — Drill drainage holes
Drill a **1/2″ hole** on the flat side of each tube, closest to the valve cover edge. This allows oil that enters the tube to drain back into the valve cover rather than being carried into the intake.

### Step 5 — Clean and reinstall
- Deburr and sand all cut and drilled edges smooth.
- Clean with solvent.
- Reinstall the tubes (no gaskets needed).
- Preserve and reinstall the metal deflector shields during reassembly.

### Step 6 — Reinstall the valve cover
Use **Felpro** valve cover gaskets.
- Torque sequence: **35 inch-pounds** initially, then **55 inch-pounds**.
