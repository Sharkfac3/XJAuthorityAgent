# Renix Injector Upgrade — Volvo 746

## Purpose
Replace the factory Renix fuel injectors with Volvo 746 four-hole injectors for improved fuel atomization, smoother idle, and better throttle response on 1987–1990 XJ and MJ vehicles.

## Problem with factory injectors
The original Renix injectors (Siemens-Deka) use a single-hole design with poor atomization. They are also prone to leaking fuel at a seam, spraying raw fuel onto hot intake manifold surfaces — a fire risk.

---

## Recommended injector

| Spec | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Volvo model | 746 |
| Bosch part number | 0 280 155 746 (also listed as 62682) |
| Spray pattern | 4-hole |
| Flow rate (factory rating) | 19.8 lb/hr at 3 bar (43.5 psi) |
| Flow rate at Renix operating pressure (31–39 psi) | ~16.7–18.7 lb/hr |
| Fitment | Direct bolt-in on Renix intake manifold |

### Injectors to avoid
- 703 injectors — not recommended for early Renix
- Unverified Chinese copies of the 746

## Installation

### Before starting
- Inspect and repair the MAP sensor vacuum line: `work/repairs/engine/renix-tb-to-map-hose-fix.md`
- A damaged MAP line during injector work is the most common cause of post-installation black smoke and rough idle.

### Steps
1. Attach injectors to the fuel rail with the clips before installation.
2. Lubricate all injector O-rings with engine oil.
3. Carefully align the injector rail to the intake manifold.
4. Push/press the rail with both hands until all injectors seat fully.
5. Fasten the rail bolts using a progressive tightening sequence — do not torque one bolt fully before others are started.
6. Reconnect the fuel line quick-disconnects. Replace O-rings if any are suspect.
   - 3/8″ quick-disconnect O-ring: NAPA 730-5018
   - 5/16″ quick-disconnect O-ring: NAPA 730-5017

### Post-installation checks
- Inspect the MAP sensor vacuum line for damage.
- Check for fuel leaks at the rail and quick-disconnects before starting.
- Start and check for vacuum leaks using carburetor cleaner spray around the intake manifold and MAP line.
