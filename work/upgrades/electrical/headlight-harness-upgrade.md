# Headlight Harness Upgrade

## Purpose
Replace the factory headlight power path with a relay-based supplemental harness that delivers full battery voltage directly to the headlamp bulbs on XJ and MJ vehicles.

## Why this matters
Factory headlight wiring runs from the battery through interior connectors, through the headlamp switch, and back out to the lamps via undersized wire. Voltage at the bulbs is typically only **10.5 volts**. The undersized wiring and repeated switching current through the headlamp switch causes switch overheating, melting, and burnout.

A relay harness delivers full battery voltage directly to the lamps while using the existing factory wiring only to trigger the relays — eliminating high current from the switch circuit and resulting in approximately **35% brighter headlamps**.

---

## Parts
- H4 two-headlamp relay harness with ceramic connectors (available on eBay)
  - Search for: "2 headlamp H4 relay harness with ceramic connectors"
  - These harnesses are often listed as not fitting Jeep — they fit.

---

## Installation

1. Remove the grille and headlamp bulbs.
2. Feed the harnesses from the passenger side, between the battery and headlamp housing, toward the driver's side.
3. Plug the driver-side bulb into the new harness.
4. Attach the driver-side harness ground wire under a small radiator support bolt after scraping paint to bare metal.
5. Route the new harness behind the grille toward the passenger side.
6. Plug the new harness into the passenger headlamp.
7. Plug the **original headlamp plug** into the receptacle on the new harness (this is the trigger signal for the relays).
8. Attach the passenger-side ground wire to the radiator support bolt (scrape to bare metal).
9. Mount the relay bracket on the passenger-side inner fender.
10. Connect the power wires to the battery.

---

## Result
Voltage at the bulbs improves from approximately 10.5V to near full battery voltage. Headlamp switch no longer carries headlamp current and will not overheat.

---

## Notes
- Four-headlamp XJ configurations (some early models) require a four-beam harness and additional pinout work. Consult Cherokee forum resources for four-lamp pinout diagrams.
- Apply OxGard to the radiator support ground contact points.
