# Renix CPS Advance Mod

## Purpose
Advance ignition timing on Renix-era (1987–1990) XJ and MJ vehicles by repositioning the sensor element within the CPS body.

## Effect
Repositioning the sensor approximately **3/8″** along the perforated bar provides roughly **5 degrees** of additional ignition advance. Best results when combined with the CPS drill mod (enlarged mounting hole).

---

## Prerequisites
- Perform `diagnostics/engine/renix-cps-test.md` first — confirm the CPS is producing adequate voltage before modifying it.
- Consider performing the mounting hole drill mod alongside this: `work/upgrades/electrical/renix-cps-drill-mod.md`

---

## Tools
- Vise
- Hammer
- Bolt that fits tightly into the top hole of the CPS perforated bar
- Paint marker or paint pen

---

## Procedure

1. Mark the **top end** of the CPS perforated bar with paint or marker as a reference point.
2. Place the CPS in a vise — clamp just lightly enough to hold it, with the sensor body resting on the vise jaws and the top of the bar protruding upward.
3. Insert a bolt that fits tightly into the hole at the top of the bar.
4. Tap the top of the bar with a hammer — the bar will slide through the sensor body.
5. Stop when the sensor body is approximately **3/8″ from the edge of the bolt hole**.

---

## Notes
- The CPS plastic housing can be brittle on older units — work carefully and do not use excessive force.
- If the bar resists moving, do not force it. A brittle housing may crack.
- This mod shifts timing advance — recheck driveability after installation.
