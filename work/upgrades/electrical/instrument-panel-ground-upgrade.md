# Instrument Panel Ground Upgrade

## Purpose
Improve the instrument panel ground on XJ and MJ vehicles by adding a supplemental jumper wire to a more reliable chassis contact point.

## Why this matters
The factory instrument panel ground contact point lacks adequate chassis conductivity. This single ground serves a large number of circuits. Poor contact causes erratic behavior across all of them simultaneously.

## Circuits served by this ground
- Dome lamps
- Seat belt and key warnings
- Transmission power/comfort switch
- Wiper switch
- Headlamp switch and delay module
- Fog lamp switch
- Cargo lamp switch
- All instrument panel grounds and illumination
- Power windows and door locks
- Cruise control dump valve

---

## Location
Lying on your back under the driver's side dash with a flashlight: look for a black wire fastened to shiny metal **almost directly above the hood release knob**, secured with a 1/4″ or 5/16″ screw head.

---

## Materials
- #10 gauge wire, approximately 10″ long (12″ for Comanche)
- 1/4″ round wire terminal (one end)
- 3/8″ round wire terminal (other end)
- Washer and nut
- OxGard

---

## Procedure

1. Crimp the 1/4″ terminal on one end of the wire and the 3/8″ terminal on the other.
2. Remove the original ground screw and attach the 1/4″ terminal alongside the original wire under the same screw. Reinstall the screw.
3. Locate the **8mm stud** above the driver's side plastic kick panel, forward of the hood release knob.
4. Attach the 3/8″ terminal to the 8mm stud with a washer and nut. Tighten securely.
5. Apply OxGard to all ground contact surfaces.

---

## Comanche (MJ) variation
Use a **12″** jumper wire. Attach the larger terminal on the driver's side kick panel close to the fusebox, on the 8mm stud in that area.
