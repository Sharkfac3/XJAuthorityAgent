# Fuel Pump Ground Upgrade

## Purpose
Add a short, direct chassis ground for the fuel pump and sending unit on XJ and MJ vehicles, bypassing the long factory ground path that runs through corrosion-prone connectors.

## Problem
The factory fuel pump ground takes a long path through connectors that are prone to corrosion and moisture:
- **XJ:** Sheet metal screw behind the spare tire
- **MJ:** Behind the driver's tail lamp

The length of the path and number of connectors in series causes voltage drop and unreliable fuel pump operation.

---

## Materials
- 14-gauge wire minimum, 12–18″ long
- Crimp eyelet connector
- Solder and soldering iron
- Electrical tape and split loom
- OxGard

---

## Procedure

1. Locate the **3-wire fuel pump/sender connector**. Work from the **harness side** — the side that belongs to the vehicle, not the pump. The correct wire is coming out of the frame toward the front of the vehicle.
2. Remove approximately 6″ of split loom covering from the black wire on the harness side.
3. Strip approximately 1/2″ of insulation from the black wire (do not cut through it).
4. Strip 3/4″ from one end of the new ground wire.
5. Wrap the new wire around the exposed section of the black harness wire.
6. Solder the connection securely.
7. Tape the splice and reinstall split loom covering.
8. Crimp an eyelet onto the opposite end of the new ground wire.
9. Find a nearby chassis bolt, clean the contact area thoroughly, apply OxGard, and attach the eyelet.

---

## Notes
- Work from the **harness side** of the connector — not the fuel pump pigtail side.
- This is an additive mod — the factory ground path is retained. The new wire provides a shorter, more reliable parallel path.
