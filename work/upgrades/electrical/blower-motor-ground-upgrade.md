# Blower Motor Ground Upgrade

## Purpose
Add a dedicated local ground for the blower motor on 1984–1990 XJ and MJ vehicles to improve blower performance and reduce load on the overloaded factory ground circuit.

## Applies to
1984–1990 XJ Cherokee and MJ Comanche.

## Problem
The factory blower motor ground is located on the **driver's side inner fender** under a sheet metal screw — approximately 10 feet from the blower motor on the passenger side. This single ground point is shared with:

- Windshield wipers
- Front and rear windshield washers
- AC clutch relay
- Fan control relay
- Fog lamps
- Fan motor
- Headlamps
- Turn signals
- Side markers
- Park lamps

The shared, distant ground degrades blower motor speed and puts all these circuits at risk from a single corroded connection.

---

## Materials
- Yellow crimp eyelet connector
- Sheet metal screw
- OxGard

---

## Procedure

### Step 1 — Locate the blower motor connector
Find the two-wire blower motor connector on the passenger side (red and black wires).

### Step 2 — Reroute the black wire
Cut the black wire and reroute the harness so the cut end can reach the **passenger side inner fender**.

### Step 3 — Crimp the eyelet
Combine both cut wire ends into a yellow eyelet and crimp securely.

### Step 4 — Prepare the mounting surface
Scrape the passenger side inner fender attachment point to bare metal.

### Step 5 — Apply OxGard
Apply OxGard to all contact surfaces before fastening.

### Step 6 — Fasten the new ground
Attach the eyelet to the passenger side inner fender with a sheet metal screw.

### Step 7 — Refresh the factory ground
Remove the original driver's side sheet metal screw ground, scrape the contact point to bare metal, and reinstall securely.

---

## Result
Improved blower motor speed and longevity. Reduced load on the shared driver's side ground circuit, improving performance of all other circuits it serves.
