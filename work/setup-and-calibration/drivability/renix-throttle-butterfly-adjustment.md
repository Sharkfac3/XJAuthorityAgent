# Renix Throttle Butterfly Adjustment

## Purpose
Set the throttle body butterfly stop screw to the correct position on Renix-era (1987–1990) XJ and MJ vehicles.

## What this screw does
This is **not** an idle adjustment screw. It is a **butterfly stop screw** — its only job is to allow the butterfly to close as completely as possible without binding against or wearing into the throttle body bore. It was never intended for field adjustment and should not be used to raise or lower idle speed.

---

## Tools
- 3/32″ allen wrench
- .003″ feeler gauge (optional but recommended — approximately the thickness of sticky-note paper)

---

## Procedure

**Engine must be off.**

1. Back out the butterfly stop screw using the 3/32″ allen wrench until the butterfly closes completely.
2. Slowly turn the screw **in** until you detect the faintest movement of the butterfly beginning to open.
3. If using a feeler gauge: set the clearance to **.003″** (sticky-note paper thickness) between the butterfly edge and the bore.
4. Do not overtighten — the butterfly must be able to close fully.

**Note:** This procedure is easier to perform with the throttle body removed from the vehicle.

### If removing the throttle body
- Clean the throttle body thoroughly before reinstalling.
- Replace the throttle body-to-manifold gasket.

### After adjustment
Readjust the TPS: `work/setup-and-calibration/drivability/renix-tps-adjustment.md`
