# 4.0 TDC Procedure — Setting #1 to Top Dead Center

## Purpose
Find #1 cylinder top dead center (TDC) on the Jeep 4.0L inline-six. Required before distributor indexing, timing verification, and any engine work that depends on a known crank position.

## Applies to
All XJ/MJ 4.0L engines across all model years.

---

## Tools
- 3/4″ socket or wrench (front crankshaft bolt)
- Chalk or white-out marker

---

## Procedure

1. Remove the #1 spark plug.
2. Mark the degree marks on the front cover and the timing mark on the harmonic balancer with chalk or white-out — makes them easier to see while rotating.
3. Using the **3/4″ front crankshaft bolt**, rotate the engine clockwise by hand.
4. Place a finger or thumb over the #1 spark plug hole while rotating.
5. As the balancer timing mark approaches the front cover marks, watch for compression pressure pushing against your finger.
   - **Pressure felt:** you are approaching #1 TDC. Continue slowly until the balancer mark aligns with the **0 mark** on the front cover.
   - **No pressure:** you are at #6 TDC. Continue rotating a full 360° until the marks come together again and compression pressure is present.
6. Stop with the balancer timing mark aligned to the **0 (TDC) mark** on the front cover.

---

## Notes
- Always rotate the engine **clockwise** (as viewed from the front) — direction matters for timing references.
- The compression pressure check is essential — the balancer passes the 0 mark twice per full engine cycle (once at #1 TDC, once at #6 TDC). They are visually identical.
