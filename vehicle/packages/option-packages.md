# XJ Cherokee Factory Option Packages

Factory option packages that materially change hardware beyond what trim level alone determines. Each package creates a distinct baseline that affects parts selection, swap planning, and diagnostics.

---

## Transfer case packages (4WD models only)

### Command-Trac — NP231

- **Type:** Part-time 4WD only
- **Positions:** 2H / 4H / Neutral / 4L
- **Years available:** 1987–2001
- **Standard on:** Most 4WD XJs; the default 4WD package

The NP231 is a chain-drive transfer case with a slip-yoke rear output. No center differential — 4H and 4L lock the front and rear driveshafts together. Must be used in 4H/4L only on loose or slippery surfaces; using on dry pavement causes drivetrain bind.

The slip-yoke rear output means the driveshaft slip joint is inside the transfer case rather than at the driveshaft. Extended lifts (over ~3") require a slip-yoke eliminator (SYE) kit and a longer driveshaft to maintain correct operating angles.

### Selec-Trac — NP242

- **Type:** Full-time / part-time switchable
- **Positions:** 2H / 4H Part-time / 4H Full-time / Neutral / 4L
- **Years available:** 1987–2001 (replaced NP228/229 from 1984–1986)
- **Available as:** Upgrade option over Command-Trac

The NP242 adds a center differential to the 4H position. "4H Full" distributes torque between axles through an open center diff — usable on dry pavement but provides no torque bias front-to-rear. "4H Part" locks the center diff for traditional locked 4WD behavior. 4L is always locked.

The NP242 also uses a slip-yoke rear output. SYE kits are available for the 242 as well.

**Identification:** Selec-Trac vehicles have a 4-position selector (or a dash switch with a "4H Full" position absent on Command-Trac trucks).

## Rear axle packages

### Trac-Lok (limited slip differential)

- **Axles:** Available on both Chrysler 8.25 (primary) and Dana 35 (less common)
- **Years:** C8.25 Trac-Lok available 1991–2001 on non-ABS models; Dana 35 Trac-Lok available on some years
- **Availability:** Factory option, not trim-specific

Trac-Lok is a clutch-pack limited slip. It biases torque to the wheel with more traction but does not fully lock. Performance degrades as clutch packs wear — a tired Trac-Lok behaves like an open differential.

**Identification:** A tag on the axle tube will reference "Trac-Lok" or the ratio code will appear with a "T" suffix. Functional test: jack both rear wheels off the ground, place trans in gear with engine off, turn one wheel — if the other turns the same direction, a limited slip is present.

## Suspension packages

### Up-Country Suspension Package

- **Years available:** Various; commonly seen on 1990s production
- **Contents:**
  - Approximately 1.5" lift over factory stock (firmer/taller springs)
  - Rear sway bar deleted
  - Front and rear skid plates
  - Front tow hooks (2) and rear tow hook (1)
  - Upgraded gas-charged shocks
  - Full-size spare tire (in place of compact spare)
  - Heavy-duty cooling (radiator and/or transmission cooler)
  - Limited slip rear differential (Trac-Lok)

**Why it matters:** An Up-Country XJ has a different suspension baseline than a stock XJ — springs are stiffer and taller, rear anti-roll characteristics are different, and the axle ratio may differ. A lift kit installed on an Up-Country vehicle starts from a higher baseline.

## Towing package

- **Years available:** 1987–2001 (contents evolved across years)
- **Contents:**
  - Class III receiver hitch
  - Trailer wiring harness (4-flat or 7-way depending on year)
  - Transmission oil cooler (external; mounted in front of radiator)
  - Heavy-duty radiator
  - 130A alternator (higher output than base)
  - Upgraded battery (higher cold-crank rating)
- **Towing capacity:** 5,000 lb with Class III hitch (factory rating)

**Early years (1987–1989) special case:** The tow package in 1987–1989 production could include a Dana 44 rear axle — the only factory-installed Dana 44 in any XJ. See [`../systems/axles/stock-specs.md`](../systems/axles/stock-specs.md).

**Why it matters for buyers:** A tow-package XJ has a transmission cooler line already installed, an upgraded alternator, and heavier cooling. These are desirable for a daily-driver or tow vehicle and are worth confirming when evaluating a used XJ.

## Cold weather / snow package

- **Also called:** Cold Weather Group, Winter Package
- **Contents (verified core items):**
  - Engine block heater (plugs into 120V outlet; warms coolant overnight)
  - Heavy-duty battery with higher cold-crank rating
- **Additional items on some year/trim combinations:** Heated front seats, heated exterior mirrors, windshield wiper de-icer

**Why it matters:** The block heater is a permanently installed component. Its presence is usually visible as a plug emerging from the grille or front bumper area. A block heater is valuable in cold climates but does not affect diagnostic or mechanical assumptions.

## Sport Appearance Package (late years)

Available on Sport trim in some years. Contents varied by year but typically included:
- Monochromatic exterior treatment (color-keyed bumpers and cladding)
- Specific wheel and tire combination
- Interior trim upgrades

No mechanical hardware differences from standard Sport trim. Does not affect axle, transfer case, or powertrain selection.
