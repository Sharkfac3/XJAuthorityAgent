# XJ Cherokee Trim Levels

## Overview

The XJ ran 18 model years with a changing trim lineup. Trim level determines interior content, appearance package, and — in some cases — which factory options were available. Trim level does **not** determine axle family (that is driven by ABS presence) or transfer case (that is driven by the 4WD option chosen).

---

## Trim level timeline

| Trim | Years | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| Base | 1984–1993 | Entry level; manual windows/locks on most; minimal content |
| SE | 1994–2001 | Replaced Base; slightly more content but still entry level |
| Pioneer | 1984–1990 | Mid-tier early trim; cloth interior, some convenience features |
| Chief (Cherokee Chief) | 1984–1989 | Appearance trim with two-tone paint and decals; dropped after 1989 |
| Wagoneer / Wagoneer Limited | 1984–1990 | Upper-tier trim sold under the Wagoneer sub-brand on the XJ platform |
| Sport | 1989–2001 | Mid trim with appearance focus; debuted 1989 alongside the Laredo |
| Laredo | 1985–1992 | Chrome grille, alloy wheels, leather seats (1992), lower body cladding; dropped when Sport/Country/Classic replaced the mid-tier |
| Briarwood | 1991–1992 | Brief two-year trim that occupied the Wagoneer's vacated slot |
| Country | 1993–1997 | Champagne-colored cladding, bumpers, and fenders; silver option from 1994; positioned for rural/towing buyers |
| Classic | 1996, 1998–2001 | Color-keyed bumpers, grille, and moldings; dark grey alloy wheels; upgraded cloth interior; optional leather and overhead console; absent in 1997 |
| Limited | 1987–2001 | Top trim throughout production; full power accessories, leather seats, overhead console (1990+) with compass and temp gauge, Infinity sound system (some years) |

## Mechanically significant trim differences

### Rear axle — driven by ABS, not trim

Trim level does not determine which rear axle a Cherokee has. ABS presence does:

- **ABS-equipped** (available as option from 1994): Dana 35 rear axle — no exceptions in stock form
- **Non-ABS** (1991–2001): Chrysler 8.25 rear axle — 27-spline 1991–1996, 29-spline 1997–2001
- **All years before 1991** (or any year without the C8.25 option): Dana 35

A high-trim Limited with ABS has a Dana 35. A base-trim SE without ABS has a C8.25. The trim badge tells you nothing about the axle.

See [`../systems/axles/overview.md`](../systems/axles/overview.md) for the full axle matrix and [`../systems/axles/identification.md`](../systems/axles/identification.md) for how to identify which axle is present.

### Power windows and locks wiring

Factory power windows and power door locks were standard on Limited and commonly available on Laredo, Classic, and upper-trim variants. Base, SE, and some Sport trims were often equipped with manual windows and locks.

**Why this matters:** Retrofitting power windows on a bare-wired Cherokee requires sourcing not just the switches and motors but the full door harness for that side. The wiring is not present in the body harness on base-trim vehicles — it was never installed from the factory.

### Air conditioning

A/C was not universally standard across any trim level — it was an option that could be present or absent on a given vehicle regardless of trim. Its presence affects:
- Underhood wiring (A/C clutch relay, pressure switch circuits)
- Compressor routing and belt arrangement
- Firewall penetration and evaporator box presence

Verify A/C presence by physical inspection, not trim badge.

### Tow package

The factory tow package was an add-on option available across multiple trims. It was not exclusive to Country or Limited trims. See [`option-packages.md`](option-packages.md) for contents.

## Trim level by era (rough groupings)

**Early production (1984–1990):** Base / Pioneer / Chief / Wagoneer / Laredo / Limited. The Wagoneer branding created a separate upper-end identity. The Chief was pure appearance trim.

**Transition years (1991–1993):** Base / Sport / Briarwood / Laredo / Limited. The Wagoneer name and Chief trim were retired; Briarwood briefly occupied the vacated upper-mid slot.

**Mid production (1993–1997):** Base (→SE in 1994) / Sport / Country / Limited. Classic appeared in 1996 briefly before disappearing in 1997. Country was the rural/towing-positioned trim.

**Late production (1998–2001):** SE / Sport / Classic / Limited. This is the most commonly seen lineup on surviving XJs. Country was dropped. Classic replaced it as the mid-tier.
