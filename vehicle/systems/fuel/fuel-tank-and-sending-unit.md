# Fuel Tank and Sending Unit

## Tank

**Capacity:** ~20 gallons on all fuel-injected 4.0L XJs in scope. Some early vehicles have a 13.5-gallon variant — this is caused by an internal baffle/vent tube configuration, not a physically different tank. Both variants are identical in external dimensions and accept the same pump hardware. Source: NAXJA, JeepForum community threads.

**Construction:** Steel tank on early XJs; plastic/composite on later years. All are in-tank pump designs.

**Tank dimensions (community-cited):** approximately 33-5/8 × 20-3/4 × 13 inches.

**Practical fuel capacity:** Running the tank to bone dry is rare — most owners report fill-ups of 15–17 gallons. The low fuel warning light typically activates around 4 gallons remaining. Running consistently below 3–4 gallons accelerates fuel pump wear, as the pump uses fuel for cooling.

## Sending unit

The sending unit is a float-and-arm assembly mounted to the fuel pump module inside the tank. As fuel level changes, the float arm moves a wiper across a resistor track, changing the resistance signal sent to the fuel gauge.

**Resistance specs (Era 1/2, 1988–1995):**
- Full: ~20 ohms
- Empty: ~220 ohms
Source: NAXJA community data (FSM-referenced values).

**Era 3 (1996–2001):** The sending unit is physically integrated with the pump module — the float arm, resistor, and pump are one assembly. Replacing the pump means replacing the sending unit, and vice versa. The 1996-specific module has different resistance values than the 1997–2001 units; parts are not cross-year interchangeable. Confirm year fitment when ordering. Source: NAXJA 1996 fuel pump thread.

## Common failures

**Float arm bent or jammed:**
- Symptom: gauge reads full or stuck at a fixed level regardless of fuel quantity
- Cause: arm bent during pump removal/installation, or float arm jammed against tank wall due to misalignment when inserting the module
- Diagnosis: remove pump, check arm movement by hand; measure resistance across sender terminals while manually moving the arm — resistance should vary smoothly across full range

**Sending unit corrosion or resistor wear:**
- Symptom: erratic gauge reading, gauge moves while sitting still, gauge reads empty with fuel in tank
- More common on high-mileage or water-contaminated tanks

**Aftermarket sending unit mismatch:**
- Documented issue with Airtex aftermarket pump modules: the sending unit resistor has fewer "fingers" on the circuit board than OEM, resulting in inaccurate gauge readings
- Carter and Bosch replacements are community-preferred for accurate gauge behavior
- Source: NAXJA, Cherokee Forum community threads

**1997 safety recall (#755):**
Some 1997 XJs built through May 9, 1997 were subject to a recall for the sending unit potentially causing the fuel gauge to display inaccurately, resulting in unexpected run-out. Source: community recall reference (JeepForum).

## Notes on tank swaps

Tank hardware is not freely cross-year compatible:
- Pre-1996 and 1996–2001 tanks use different pump modules and regulator setups
- The sending unit resistor values differ by era — mixing a pre-96 sender with a 96+ gauge (or vice versa) will result in wrong readings
- ZJ (Grand Cherokee) tanks have been swapped into XJs by the community for greater capacity (~23 gallons); requires correct pump module fitment for the destination year's fuel system
