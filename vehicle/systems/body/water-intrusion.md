# XJ Water Intrusion — Stock Leak Points

## Purpose
Document where water enters the XJ cabin from factory-original failure modes. This is a stock-facts reference. For symptom-first isolation ("I have wet carpet"), see [`diagnostics/body-and-interior/water-intrusion.md`](../../../diagnostics/body-and-interior/water-intrusion.md).

Water intrusion is a primary driver of floor pan and rocker rust on the XJ. Because the XJ is a unibody, rust that begins as a water intrusion problem becomes a structural problem. See [`overview.md`](overview.md).

---

## Common leak points

### Windshield seal

The windshield is bonded to the body with a rubber seal that dries and cracks with age. Cracks at the lower corners are the most common failure point. Water enters here and runs down the A-pillar behind the dash or headliner, pooling at the driver's and passenger's footwells.

**How it presents:** Wet carpet at the front corners of the cab, often with no obvious visible leak path. The A-pillar trim may show water staining or mold.

**Source basis:** Widely documented on Cherokee Forum and NAXJA as a primary front floor intrusion source.

---

### Firewall grommets

Wire harness bundles and HVAC components pass through the firewall via rubber grommets. These grommets harden and crack with age, or pull away from the firewall opening, leaving a gap. Water can enter through any unsealed penetration.

The HVAC system also routes a drain through the firewall base. If the heater box drain becomes clogged, condensation and rainwater back up and overflow into the passenger footwell.

**How it presents:** Wet passenger footwell, sometimes with a pattern consistent with HVAC drain overflow rather than a seam leak.

---

### Tailgate seal

The upper hatch (liftgate glass) and the lower tailgate both have perimeter seals. The upper hatch seal is the more common failure — it dries, hardens, and separates from the body. Water then runs down the interior of the rear hatch opening and collects on the rear floor.

Body seam cracks at the top of the tailgate opening have also been reported and contribute to rear interior water.

**How it presents:** Wet rear cargo floor and/or wet rear passenger footwells; water staining on the rear interior trim panels.

---

### Sunroof (if equipped)

Some XJs were equipped from the factory with a sliding sunroof. The sunroof panel drains through channels that exit via rubber tubes routed through the body. These drain tubes:
- Clog with debris over time
- Detach from the drain fitting, routing water directly into the headliner cavity

Rear drain tubes are the more common failure point. When the drains are blocked or detached, water pools in the sunroof tray and overflows into the headliner.

**How it presents:** Wet headliner, water staining or drips near the sunroof opening, wet front floor if water tracks forward along the headliner.

---

### Door seals

All four doors use rubber perimeter weatherstripping that contacts the door opening. These seals compress and harden with age and use. A seal that no longer makes full contact along its length allows water to enter the door cavity and potentially the cab.

Compounding factor: each door has drain holes at the bottom of the door cavity designed to let any water that enters exit at the bottom. These drain holes clog with debris. When they clog, water pools in the door cavity and causes rust through the door skin. If the interior seal between the door cavity and cab is also degraded, water enters the footwell.

**How it presents:** Wet front floor at the A-pillar base; water inside the door when the door panel is removed; rust at door bottoms. See [`rust-locations.md`](rust-locations.md).

---

## Why this matters on a unibody

Water intrusion on a body-on-frame vehicle eventually causes rust that is mostly cosmetic or affects replaceable components. On a unibody like the XJ, water that pools on the floor pan causes floor pan rust, which is adjacent to the rocker panels, which are structural members. The failure chain is:

1. Leak point fails (windshield seal, grommets, etc.)
2. Water pools under carpet on floor pan
3. Floor pan develops surface rust, then scale, then rust-through
4. Rocker panels rust from inside due to trapped moisture
5. Rocker rust-through compromises A-pillar and B-pillar base attachment

This is not a hypothetical progression — it is the standard XJ rust pattern seen on high-mileage examples from the rust belt and wet climates.
