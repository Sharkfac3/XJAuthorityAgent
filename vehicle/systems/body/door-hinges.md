# XJ Door Hinges

## Purpose
Document door hinge wear on the XJ: what it is, how to identify it, and how to fix it.

This is a maintenance and wear item, not a structural concern. It is documented here because worn hinges affect door sealing, which is a contributing factor to water intrusion and the rust that follows.

---

## Background

Door hinge pin wear is a common issue on high-mileage XJs. Each door uses two hinges welded to the A-pillar or B-pillar. Each hinge consists of a cast-iron hinge body and a steel pivot pin. There is no factory grease fitting or lube provision — the pins run dry from the factory.

Over years and high mileage, the steel pin wears against the cast-iron hinge body. The result is slop in the hinge, which allows the door to sag on its hinges.

---

## Symptoms

- Door sags visibly — the gap at the top of the door leading edge is wider than the gap at the bottom
- Door won't latch cleanly, or requires lifting the door slightly to engage the latch
- Door check strap (the rubber-tipped stop rod) contacts or binds against the door opening
- Door seal does not seat evenly around the full perimeter

Any of these symptoms can also be caused by a bent or misaligned door or striker plate adjustment, so verify the hinge pins are actually worn before replacing them.

**How to verify hinge wear:** Open the door to about 45 degrees. Grasp the door at the outer lower corner and try to move it up and down. Healthy hinges will show no movement. Worn hinge pins will allow 1/4" or more of vertical movement at the door corner. The movement will be visible at the hinge gap.

---

## Fix

**Option 1: Pin and bushing rebuild kit**

Aftermarket kits provide new steel pins and nylon or brass bushings that press into the hinge body. This restores the original pivot geometry without replacing the hinge assembly.

- Preferred source: Mopar replacement pins and bushings, or equivalent quality aftermarket kits
- Note: Some aftermarket kits have pins with a larger diameter than the factory hole — verify fitment before purchase
- The job requires driving out the old pin with a punch and hammer, pressing in new bushings, and driving in the new pin

**Option 2: Full hinge replacement**

If the hinge body itself is wallowed out beyond what a pin kit can correct, the hinge assembly must be replaced. Factory hinges are welded to the pillar; replacement typically means cutting off the old hinge and welding a new one in position, or using bolt-on replacement hinges where the pillar hole is drilled.

---

## Classification

Door hinge wear is a **wear and maintenance item**, not a structural concern. The hinges are not load-bearing members of the unibody.

However, worn hinges allow the door to sit unevenly in the opening, which compresses the door seal unevenly. An uneven or poorly seating door seal allows water intrusion into the door cavity. If the door drain holes are clogged, water then pools in the door bottom, causing rust through the door skin. More importantly, water can enter the cab through the door seal and reach the floor pan. See [`water-intrusion.md`](water-intrusion.md).
