# XJ Body Overview

## Purpose
Stock XJ body architecture, structural baseline, and branch router.

---

## Unibody construction

The Jeep Cherokee XJ (1984–2001) uses **unibody construction**. There is no separate ladder frame. The body shell is the structure — the two are one integrated unit that cannot be separated.

This distinguishes the XJ from:
- The YJ/TJ/JK Wrangler, which uses a separate body bolted to a ladder frame
- The full-size Cherokee (SJ), which also used a body-on-frame design

**Why this matters:** On a body-on-frame vehicle, rust that damages the body panels is largely cosmetic. The frame is a separate, inspectable component. On a unibody, rust in the body panels IS rust in the frame. There is no separate structural baseline underneath. Rust assessment on an XJ is structural assessment.

---

## Key structural members

These are the components whose integrity directly determines whether the vehicle is structurally safe:

**Rocker panels (sills)**
Full-length box sections running the base of the body from front wheel opening to rear wheel opening. The A-pillar and B-pillar both anchor at their bases to the rockers. The B-pillar carries the seatbelt anchor. Rust-through at the rockers compromises pillar attachment and the vehicle's crash structure.

**Rear frame rails**
Box-section members under the cargo floor, running rearward from behind the rear seat to the shackle hangers where the rear leaf springs attach. These are the most critical single rust location on the XJ. They are the rearward anchor of the unibody and the attachment point for the rear suspension.

**Floor pans**
The floor pans contribute to the overall rigidity of the tub. They are not as critical as rockers or rear rails, but rust-through floor pans are adjacent to rocker structures and accelerate rocker decay through trapped moisture.

**Firewall**
The forward structural bulkhead separating the engine bay from the cab. Provides rigidity at the front of the passenger cell. Rust at the firewall lower section is primarily a water-intrusion indicator rather than an immediate structural failure mode.

**A-pillar and B-pillar**
The vertical pillars flanking the front and rear door openings. Structural in the context of roof load and side-impact resistance. Both anchor to the rocker at the base — their structural integrity depends on the rocker condition beneath them.

---

## Age context

The XJ was produced from 1984 to 2001. In 2026, the youngest possible XJ is 25 years old. The oldest is 42 years old. At this age, **rust is the primary structural risk factor** on any XJ from a high-rainfall or road-salt climate. A mechanically excellent XJ with compromised rear frame rails or rusted-through rockers is not a safe vehicle and may not be a cost-effective repair candidate.

This is a different calculus than evaluating a modern vehicle or a body-on-frame truck. Rust assessment must happen before any other evaluation on a prospective purchase.
