# AX-15 Manual Transmission

## Identity
- Manufacturer: Aisin (Japan)
- Type: 5-speed manual, fully synchronized, all gears helically cut
- XJ years: mid-1989 through 1999 (behind 4.0L I6)
- Replaced by: NV3550 in 2000–2001 SE trim only; most late XJs still have the AX-15
- Also used in: YJ and TJ Wrangler, MJ Comanche; one year in ZJ Grand Cherokee (1993, 6-cyl only)
- Paired engine: 4.0L I6 exclusively in XJ
- Case length: approximately 16.5 inches, cast aluminum with ribbed case and rear-quarter shifter

The AX-15 replaced the BA-10/5 Peugeot unit mid-production-year 1989 (approximately March 1989). It is widely regarded as the most desirable factory manual for the XJ.

---

## Gear ratios

| Gear | Ratio |
|------|-------|
| 1st | 3.83:1 |
| 2nd | 2.33:1 |
| 3rd | 1.44:1 |
| 4th | 1.00:1 |
| 5th (OD) | 0.79:1 |
| Reverse | 4.22:1 |

Stock crawl ratio with AX-15 and NP231 (2.72 low range) and 3.07 axle gears: approximately 32:1. Stock AW4 XJs were typically geared 3.55 in the axles; AX-15 XJs typically came with 3.07 axle gears.

---

## Output shaft

- Spline count: 23-spline output shaft
- Mates to 23-spline input on NP231 or NP242 transfer cases (1992+)

---

## Slave cylinder split

- Through 1994: internal slave/throwout assembly inside the bellhousing
- 1995+: external slave cylinder and cross-arm

This matters for clutch service and when sourcing replacement parts.

---

## Fluid specification

The correct fluid varies by production year.

**1997 and newer AX-15:** GL-3 or GL-4 75W90 gear oil. GL-5 should be avoided — its high sulfur content can attack the yellow metal synchronizer components. Community-recommended fluids: Redline MT-90 (GL-4), Amsoil MTG (GL-4).

**1996 and older AX-15:** Chrysler's original spec was SAE 75W90 GL-5, and some early factory documentation lists 10W-40 motor oil as acceptable. The community has largely moved toward GL-4 75W90 for all years, with Redline MT-90 being the most widely recommended fluid across the XJ forums.

Do not use Redline's Synchromax in an AX-15 — it is too light (roughly ATF weight) and does not provide adequate protection for this gearbox.

---

## Known strengths

The AX-15 has a strong reputation across the XJ community. Behind a stock or mildly built 4.0L, failures are uncommon. It handles moderate off-road and trail use well and is considered significantly more durable than the BA-10/5 it replaced.

---

## Known failure points

**Input shaft bearing noise** — A worn input shaft bearing produces a bearing whine or rumble that changes or disappears when the clutch is depressed. Common on high-mileage units.

**Countershaft bearing wear** — Related to high mileage or lubrication neglect. Produces gear noise across multiple gears.

**5th gear wear** — 5th gear is the most lightly loaded in the geartrain but can show wear on very high-mileage transmissions. Pop-out of 5th is uncommon but documented.

The AX-15 is generally considered robust enough that rebuild is worthwhile when it does fail, unlike the BA-10/5.

---

## Swap context

The AX-15 is the standard community swap target when a BA-10/5 fails. The bellhousing pattern is compatible with the 4.0L but an adapter or bellhousing may be required depending on the source vehicle. See `work/swaps/` for swap execution guidance.
