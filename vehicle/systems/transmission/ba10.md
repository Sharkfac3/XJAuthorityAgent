# BA-10/5 Manual Transmission (Peugeot)

## Identity
- Manufacturer: Peugeot (France)
- Type: 5-speed manual
- XJ years: 1987 through mid-1989 (phased out approximately March 1989; AX-15 phased in)
- Paired engine: 4.0L I6 (also used behind the AMC 258 I6 in the YJ Wrangler)
- Common name in XJ community: "the Peugeot" or "BA10"

---

## Physical identification

The BA-10/5 is identifiable by its **split-case design** — the aluminum case is split lengthwise and the two halves are bolted together, so a visible seam runs along the bottom of the case. The AX-15 and AX-5 are solid-case designs that can only be opened at the ends.

Additional identification: the shift tower is secured to the transmission with **3 bolts**. The AX-15 uses 4 bolts.

---

## Bellhousing

The BA-10/5 has a **unique bellhousing bolt pattern** that is not shared with any other Jeep transmission. This means the bellhousing cannot be transferred to an AX-15 swap without the correct adapter or replacement bellhousing. Novak Conversions and others offer the necessary hardware for an AX-15 swap.

---

## Fluid specification

Community consensus: **Redline 75W90 NS** (Non-Synthetic or the NS designation in Redline's lineup). Standard 75W90 GL-4 or GL-5 is also used; no strong community consensus against either unlike the AX-15's GL-5 sensitivity.

**Critical: fill from the correct plug.** A well-documented failure mode is underfilling caused by servicing the fluid from the 5th-gear switch access hole rather than the proper fill plug. An underfilled BA-10/5 wears significantly faster. Confirm the correct fill point before servicing.

---

## Known failure modes

The BA-10/5 has a poor reputation in the XJ community. Documented failure modes include:

**5th gear pop-out** — The transmission drops out of 5th gear under deceleration at highway speed. Common on worn units. A persistent complaint at higher mileage.

**1st, 2nd, and reverse synchro wear** — Synchros wear over time, producing grinding or resistance when shifting into 1st, 2nd, or reverse. Cold-weather shifting difficulty (particularly 2nd gear) is normal behavior that worsens as synchros wear.

**Input shaft damage** — Bent or worn input shafts are a documented failure. If the input shaft is found damaged during a rebuild, other internal components are often compromised as well, making the repair bill significant.

**Internal noise / thunking** — A rhythmic knocking or banging sensation between the bellhousing and shifter area can indicate something damaged inside the case contacting the housing.

**Expensive to rebuild** — Replacement parts for the BA-10/5 are scarce. The only other common application is the Peugeot 505 sedan. Community consensus is that a rebuild cost often justifies swapping to an AX-15 instead.

---

## Durability notes

Despite its reputation, many BA-10/5 units have survived 200,000+ miles in stock vehicles. The transmission is adequate for stock power levels and light use. The weak points become apparent under abuse, neglect (especially underfilling), or when parts wear and rebuild becomes necessary. Some owners report smooth shifting on well-maintained examples.

---

## Swap context

The AX-15 is the community-standard replacement when a BA-10/5 fails or is being proactively replaced. The 4.0L engine block requires a compatible bellhousing — the BA-10/5's unique bellhousing does not transfer. Novak Conversions and other suppliers provide swap kits.

The NV3550 is a less common alternative swap, also supported by Novak.
