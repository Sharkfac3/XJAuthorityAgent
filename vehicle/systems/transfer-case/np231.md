# NP231 Transfer Case

## Identity
- Manufacturer: New Process Gear (later New Venture Gear)
- Brand name: Command-Trac
- Type: Part-time only (no full-time mode)
- Low range ratio: 2.72:1
- High range ratio: 1.00:1
- Drive: Chain-driven
- Case material: Aluminum
- XJ years: 1987–2001

The NP231 is the most common XJ transfer case. It is standard equipment on 4WD XJs that did not have the SelecTrac (NP242) option. Aftermarket support is extensive.

---

## Input shaft

| Years | Splines |
|-------|---------|
| 1987–1991 | 21-spline |
| 1992–2001 | 23-spline |

The input shaft must match the output shaft of the transmission. Most late XJs (1992+) have the 23-spline configuration on both the AW4 and AX-15.

---

## Rear output — slip yoke

The NP231 uses a **slip-yoke rear output** in stock form. The rear driveshaft yoke slides on a splined output shaft inside the tailhousing. This is a normal factory arrangement at stock ride height.

**The slip yoke is a vibration source on lifted XJs.** When the vehicle is lifted, rear driveshaft angles increase. The slip yoke's sliding travel keeps the shaft at adequate length, but the increased angle puts the rear U-joints into a bind that causes driveline vibration — typically felt as a shudder or buzz at highway speed. The taller the lift, the worse the vibration.

---

## Slip Yoke Eliminator (SYE)

The standard fix for slip-yoke vibration on lifted XJs is an SYE kit combined with a custom-length rear driveshaft. The SYE replaces the tailhousing and output shaft arrangement with a fixed-yoke output, allowing a proper CV driveshaft to be run at the correct angle.

**SYE types:**

**HD (Heavy Duty) SYE** — Replaces the internal output mainshaft as well as the tailhousing. The replacement shaft is typically 32-spline and significantly stronger than the OEM output. This is the preferred option. The HD SYE shortens the transfer case by approximately 4 inches, requiring a shorter custom rear driveshaft. Hardware from Advance Adapters, JB Conversions, Rubicon Express, and others.

**Hack-and-Tap (HnT) SYE** — The stock output shaft is cut shorter and drilled/tapped to accept a bolted-on driveshaft flange. Less expensive (roughly $150 less than HD SYE), adequate for moderate lifts on 1996+ XJs. Does not replace the internal mainshaft, so it is not as strong as the HD option.

Community guidance: an SYE is recommended for lifts exceeding approximately 4 inches. Below 4 inches, vibration may be minimal or absent depending on the specific lift geometry.

---

## Chain wear

The NP231 uses a chain to connect the input to the front and rear output shafts. Chain wear is normal over high mileage. When the case is opened for any reason (SYE install, seal replacement), inspect the magnet — heavy metal particle accumulation indicates accelerated internal wear and the chain and bearings should be inspected or replaced.

---

## Fluid specification

**ATF** — the community consensus on NAXJA and JeepForum is ATF+4 for the NP231. Capacity is approximately 1.5 quarts. Do not use gear oil.
