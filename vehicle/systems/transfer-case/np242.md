# NP242 Transfer Case

## Identity
- Manufacturer: New Process Gear (later New Venture Gear; also designated NVG242)
- Brand name: SelecTrac (Command-Trac II in some documentation)
- Type: Full-time capable with part-time option
- Low range ratio: 2.72:1
- High range ratio: 1.00:1
- Drive: Chain-driven
- Case material: Aluminum
- XJ years: 1987–2001 (SelecTrac option; less common than NP231)

The NP242 replaced the NP228/NP229 in 1987 as the SelecTrac transfer case. Very few mechanical changes were made throughout its production run (1987–2001 in XJ). The NP242 is heavier than the NP231 by approximately 20 lbs and hangs approximately 1 inch lower.

---

## Operating modes

The NP242 offers four modes:

| Mode | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **2H** | 2WD high range — rear wheels only |
| **4H Part-Time** | 4WD high range, locked front/rear — no center differential. Do not use on dry or paved surfaces. |
| **4H Full-Time** | 4WD high range with open center differential — safe on all surfaces |
| **4L** | 4WD low range, locked front/rear — requires slowing to 2–4 mph and transmission in neutral to engage |

**Shift-on-the-fly:** Transitions between 2H, 4H Part-Time, and 4H Full-Time can be made at road speeds up to approximately 55 mph.

**4L engagement:** 4L is the only mode transition that requires slowing nearly to a stop with the transmission in neutral. This applies to both entering and exiting 4L.

---

## Full-Time mode — when to use it

The 4H Full-Time mode uses an **open center differential** (not a viscous coupler — that is the NP249 found in ZJ/WJ). This means:
- Front and rear axles can rotate at different speeds — no drivetrain wind-up on pavement
- Safe on mixed surfaces: wet roads, ice, snow, dirt
- On slippery surfaces with no axle lockers, raising one wheel will cause it to spin — the other axle retains drive capability through differential action

**Do not use 4H Part-Time on dry or paved roads.** Without a center differential, the locked front/rear torque split causes drivetrain bind and premature wear on driveline components.

## Input shaft

| Years | Length | Splines |
|-------|--------|---------|
| 1987–1991 | 0.750" | 21-spline |
| 1992–1995 | 0.750" | 23-spline |
| 1996–2001 | 0.840" | 23-spline |

The input shaft must match the transmission output shaft. 1992+ XJs are 23-spline on both the AW4 and AX-15.

---

## Rear output — slip yoke

Like the NP231, the NP242 uses a **slip-yoke rear output** in stock form. The same driveshaft vibration problem occurs on lifted NP242-equipped XJs.

SYE kits exist for the NP242 but aftermarket support is significantly more limited than for the NP231. This is worth considering when planning a lift.

**Output shaft: 27 splines** (Jeep version). The output shaft is described by Novak as a known strength limitation of the Jeep 242 — the shaft diameter is smaller than heavy-duty applications.

**Slip yoke type by year:**
- 1990 and earlier: internal slip yoke
- 1991–1995: internal slip yoke, 23-spline input
- 1996–2001: external slip yoke, 23-spline input

---

## Fluid specification

**ATF** — any brand/type of automatic transmission fluid, approximately 1.5 quarts. Do not use gear oil.

## Seal part numbers (reference, National brand)
- Rear output seal: 4370N (shared with NP231)
- Front output seal: 3946
- Input shaft seal (single lip): 3173 (shared with NP231)
- Input shaft seal (dual lip): 710928 (shared with NP231)

## Compatibility note

When replacing an NP242, the only feasible swap candidates are NP231 or NP242 cases from an XJ or ZJ. Dodge transfer cases use different main shaft and tail cone geometry. WJ cases lack the speedometer sensor provision. See `work/swaps/transfer-case/` for swap guidance.
