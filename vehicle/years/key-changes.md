# XJ Cherokee Year-by-Year Key Changes

Mechanically significant changes by model year. Focus is on changes that affect parts compatibility, ECU behavior, common failure patterns, or buyer/swap decisions. Cosmetic-only changes are omitted.

For era-level architecture (Renix vs. SBEC vs. JTEC), see the era overview files linked in [`index.md`](index.md).

---

## 1984–1990 (pre-4.0L HO, pre-SBEC)

| Year | Change |
|------|--------|
| 1984 | First XJ Cherokee. Carbureted 2.5L I4 (base) or carbureted 2.8L GMV6. Both 2-door and 4-door body styles available from launch. NP228/229 Selec-Trac transfer case available. Dana 35 rear axle on all models. |
| 1985 | Laredo trim added. |
| 1986 | MJ Comanche pickup introduced on XJ platform (out of scope). |
| 1987 | 4.0L Renix MPI introduced (173 hp). AW4 4-speed automatic introduced alongside 4.0L. Selec-Trac updated to NP242. Limited trim introduced. Dana 44 rear axle available on tow-package XJs (1987–1989 only). |
| 1988 | Renix 4.0L revised to 177 hp (injector and tune changes). BA10/5 5-speed manual available behind 4.0L. AW4 becomes electronically controlled. |
| 1989 | Sport trim introduced. Cherokee Chief trim dropped. |
| 1990 | Wagoneer sub-brand on XJ platform discontinued. Pioneer trim last year. |

## 1991–1995 (Era 2: SBEC / OBD-I)

| Year | Change |
|------|--------|
| 1991 | **Major transition.** Renix ECU replaced by Chrysler SBEC (Era 2 begins). HO 4.0L introduced: 190 hp, roller rockers, revised head design, different CPS sensor, different O2 sensor location. AX15 5-speed manual standardized behind 4.0L. Chrysler 8.25 rear axle introduced on non-ABS 4WD models (27-spline). Briarwood trim replaces Wagoneer slot. |
| 1992 | Laredo trim last year. Briarwood last year. |
| 1993 | CPS (crankshaft position sensor) connector changes from round multi-pin to flat pin — affects sensor sourcing and wiring repair. Country trim introduced. Wagoneer and Briarwood trims dropped. |
| 1994 | SE trim replaces Base. ABS available as factory option — **ABS-equipped XJs receive Dana 35 rear axle; non-ABS XJs retain C8.25.** This ABS/axle correlation is absolute throughout all remaining production years. AX5 manual (used behind 2.5L) discontinued. |
| 1995 | Dana 30 front axle inner U-joints upgraded from 260 to 297 size on ABS-equipped models — 297s are stronger and are a recommended upgrade on pre-1995 axles. |

## 1996–2001 (Era 3: JTEC / OBD-II)

| Year | Change |
|------|--------|
| 1996 | **Major transition.** JTEC ECU replaces SBEC (Era 3 / OBD-II begins). Returnless fuel system (no return line from fuel rail to tank). Fuel pressure raised to ~49 psi (up from ~31 psi on Renix/SBEC). NVH block casting. OBD-II ALDL port. **C8.25 spline crossover:** 1996 production contains both 27-spline and 29-spline C8.25 axle shafts — mid-year production change with no VIN-derivable distinction. Physical inspection required to determine spline count. Classic trim introduced briefly. |
| 1997 | Head coolant temp sender eliminated; single coolant sensor remains at thermostat housing. C8.25 standardized to 29-spline from start of model year (no more 27-spline new production). Classic trim absent this year. |
| 1998 | Classic trim returns and runs through end of production. |
| 1999 | **"Good intake" curved runner manifold introduced.** The 1999–2001 manifold has individual runners of near-equal length and a larger plenum — provides improved high-RPM flow vs. the right-angle-bend earlier manifold. Direct bolt-on replacement for 1996+ XJs (same power steering pump mounting). 1991–1995 XJs require a power steering pump and bracket swap to use this manifold. |
| 2000 | **Dana 30 front axle geometry change:** high-pinion reverse-cut (1984–1999) replaced by low-pinion standard-cut design. The high-pinion Dana 30 ran on the stronger drive side of the gear teeth; the 2000+ low-pinion is geometrically weaker. This change affects gear swap parts sourcing — 1999-and-earlier high-pinion gearsets do not interchange with 2000+ low-pinion housings. **DIS coil pack ignition:** distributor replaced by a 3-coil strip mounted directly over the spark plugs (waste-spark configuration). No distributor cap or rotor. All 4.0L models. 2.5L I4 discontinued after this model year. Front fascia and interior revised (last significant styling update before end of production). |
| 2001 | **Final year XJ.** Largest stock intake plenum (minor revision over 1999–2000 manifold). Only 4.0L engine available — 2.5L dropped. DIS coil pack ignition continues. Dana 30 low-pinion front continues. Chrysler 8.25 (29-spline) available on non-ABS models; Dana 35 on ABS-equipped models. **No factory rear disc brakes on any 2001 XJ** — rear drum brakes on all production units. (Rear disc conversions are a common aftermarket modification using KJ Liberty or ZJ Grand Cherokee hardware.) Considered by many buyers to have the best combination of powertrain components (largest intake, coil ignition, 29-spline C8.25 on non-ABS) among all XJ years. |

## Quick reference: parts compatibility boundaries

| Boundary | What changes |
|----------|-------------|
| 1990 → 1991 | ECU family (Renix → SBEC), engine (flat tappet → roller rockers), rear axle option (C8.25 added) |
| 1993 | CPS connector (round → flat pin) — sensor not directly interchangeable |
| 1995 → 1996 | ECU family (SBEC → JTEC), fuel system (return → returnless), fuel pressure (31 → 49 psi) |
| 1996 crossover | C8.25 spline count (27 → 29 mid-year) — cannot determine from VIN |
| 1999 → 2000 | Dana 30 pinion (high → low) — gear sets do not interchange between housing generations |
| 1999 | Intake manifold generation — 1999+ is bolt-on for 1996+ only |
| 1999 → 2000 | Ignition system (distributor → coil pack) |
